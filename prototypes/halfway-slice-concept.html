Hero's Compass

Design prototype — "the elephant, made eatable"

Three zoom levels. Onboarding plants the direction. The Compass cultivates it into bite-sized action. The session arc walks the first half of the year, one job at a time — until the elephant is half-eaten.

Zoom 1

Onboarding plants — light

The halfway step: your year and your "right now" on top, your wheel below, one line per slice. Nothing forced.

Zoom 3

The session arc — cutting the elephant in half

The first half of the year is Sessions 1–4, and each session has a job. Reach the halfway point and half the elephant is already eaten — a skill at a time.

First half — one goal, four sessions carried
How it ladders: a daily task (“one math skill”) → a weekly step → a session goal (the job of that session) → the half-year. You only ever hold the small end. The big end takes care of itself.

The accountability layer

Who holds you to it

Accountability scales with the size of the commitment. The daily list stays private; the big crossings get witnessed — never the other way around.

Daily tasks
You alone — plus the two doors. A partner watching your undone daily list would be surveillance. Daily stays yours.
Session crossings
finish line · halfway
Your accountability partner — witnesses & celebrates. The “someone saw me do the big thing” moment.
Year goals · pitch
Partner confirms + guide signs — the check-off flow that already exists, at the right grain.
Shared wins
Parents (opt-in) — the “share this win with my parents” moment.
Sparks · ages 4–7
The parent holds all of it — no account, no peer partner. The parent sets the aims, companions the ~2 daily things, and celebrates. This whole surface is the parent's view. 💛

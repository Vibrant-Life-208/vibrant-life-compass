# Parent Listening Protocol - True Play Badge (Step 1)

**Date:** 2026-07-16 · **Captain:** Europa / Ms. Erin · Recorded by Claude Code
**Purpose:** Unblock Picard's condition (a) from the 2026-07-09 critical review -
*"we have not talked to the people this is for."* Nothing downstream (door, videos,
reframe, guide seat) advances until this is no longer true.

**This is listening, not a pitch.** The single most important discipline: we are here
to find out whether this should exist and what it should be - NOT to validate a thing
already designed. A parent must be able to say "I don't want this" and have that be a
complete, honored answer, not an objection to overcome.

---

## Who and how

- **Who:** 3-5 parents from the 44 Parents & Tots families. Range across: newer vs
  longer-tenured, the parent who seems to be thriving AND the parent who seems to be
  struggling (the struggling parent's read matters most - the design's risk is shame).
- **Setting:** In person or voice, unhurried, no screen between you if possible. Not a
  form. Not a survey link. A conversation.
- **Length:** 20-30 min. Stop early if it wants to stop.
- **Consent + framing (say this first):** "We're thinking about a small, private
  recognition for parents - the kind of holding you already do. Before we build any of
  it, we want to know if it's even wanted, and what would make it feel wrong. There's no
  right answer, and 'I don't want anything like this' is genuinely one of the answers
  we're listening for. Nothing you say is scored or shared."
- **Recording:** Only with explicit permission. Otherwise take notes after, from memory,
  in the parent's own words where you can.

---

## The questions (open, non-leading)

Order matters: ask about their life FIRST, the artifact LAST. If you describe the badge
early, you contaminate every answer after it.

**A. Their world (before any mention of the badge)**
1. How does your family already do goodbyes - at drop-off, at bedtime, when you part?
2. Tell me about a time your little one was really playing. What were you doing?
3. When did your OWN emotions last come out to play? (Sit with the silence. Don't rescue it.)
4. What's the hardest part of the mornings / the partings for you?

**B. The felt need (still no artifact)**
5. When you're doing the hard holding work - the drop-off where they cling - does
   anyone see it? Does being seen matter, or not really?
6. Is there anything in the app right now that makes you feel behind, or measured, or
   like you're not doing enough? (Listen hard here - this is the live-arc harm.)

**C. Only now, the artifact - described plainly, once**
7. "One idea: a small private card that names a posture you already practice - like
   'the safe base' - with no score, no count, nobody else can see it, and it never says
   you're behind. It's yours or you ignore it." What's your honest first reaction?
8. What would make that feel like pressure or like one more thing to do?
9. Would a short video - a few minutes on why play matters - help, or would it feel like
   one more screen when you're already tired? (The screens-contradict-play test, asked
   of the actual parent.)
10. If we built none of this and just left you and your kid alone - would anything be lost?

**D. Close**
11. What did I not ask that I should have?
12. Is there anything you'd want us to change or stop?

---

## What we are listening FOR (the signals that change the design)

- **Kill signals** (any one is decisive, log it loudly):
  - "It would make me feel like I'm being graded / watched / not enough."
  - "I'd rather you just left us alone" said with relief, not politeness.
  - The struggling parent reads the recognition as one more failure surface.
- **Reshape signals:**
  - Being seen matters, but only if it's spoken aloud by a real guide, not an app card.
  - The video is wanted OR is clearly a burden (settles the §3 screens question).
  - The live arc's "Session N of 7" is actively making someone feel behind (→ Step 2 now).
- **Green signals (hold skeptically - beware designing-at-parents confirmation):**
  - "That would feel kind." Ask: kind enough to open, or kind enough to be polite about?

---

## Anti-patterns (do not do)

- Do NOT explain the design's cleverness (the door grammar, the mosaic, the SDT
  quadrant). The parent doesn't need our craftsmanship; we need their reality.
- Do NOT count responses or turn this into an N. Five honest conversations, reported in
  their own words. No dashboard. (Same wall as the badge itself.)
- Do NOT treat "no" as an objection to handle. It's data, and possibly the right answer.
- Do NOT lead with "play is so important" - that plants the shame we're testing for.

---

## After: what to produce

A short written synthesis in the parents' own words (quotes, not metrics):
- Should this exist? (Include "no badge" as a live verdict.)
- What must change before anything is built.
- Whether the live arc is causing harm now (→ Step 2 priority).
- Whether videos help or burden.

Then, and only then, v0.3 design deepening becomes real (or is set down).

*"We evoke - we never extract."*

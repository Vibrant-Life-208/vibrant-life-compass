## The container before the ladder *(Salus — cycle 2)*

**Before any goal: safety and trust.** A learner must feel safe before they are asked to *want.* The room is safe before the conversation; the container holds before anyone is asked to enter it. Goal-setting for a distressed or unsafe learner is not first a goal problem — it is a safety problem. *(Trauma-informed practice: safety/trust before achievable goals.)*

## 0. The fork at the top — before any breakdown

**Every goal is first *doing* or *becoming*.**
- **Doing (finish-shaped)** — the ladder in §1-7 applies.
- **Becoming (presence — Heart / Spirit / Voice / the Grows side of any region)** — **none** of the ladder. No halfway, no milestones, no weekly quota, no completion badge. Presence only: the destination held, the *noticing* below it, and **nothing sequence-shaped between them.** (Ratified becoming carve-out; per-goal track flag.)
- **"Not right now" is a real answer** — a learner may decline or pause a goal this season and be **whole.** *(Ported from the Kitchen Table's present mode.)*
- **If a learner is disengaged, find the root cause first** *(cycle 2, RQ6):* disengagement often masks **anxiety or depression, not laziness.** The move is **diagnosis before motivation** — and *come alongside* (motivational-interviewing: reframe, emphasize autonomy, elicit the learner's own reasons), **never push.** Pushing a disengaged child produces sustain-talk; walking beside them produces change-talk.

## 1. The destination — the learner's own goal, held as *inclusive autonomy* *(Kira — cycle 2)*

The big goal is the **learner's to author** — but "author your own life" does **not** mean individualist self-expression. It means **acting from what *you* value — which may be your family's, your community's, your tradition's.** *(SDT "inclusive autonomy": "my family and I" as the subject; it carries *greater* wellbeing significance in collectivist cultures, not less.)* Frame the needle as **"what we value, as I hold it,"** not "what I want, alone." This is how the compass works for a collectivist child without abandoning sovereignty.

- **Protect the big goal from imposition** *(cycle 2, RQ1):* parental **control on big dreams is more damaging than on daily tasks.** So the **needle / year goal is the learner's to author** — a parent or guide may help with logistics, timing, and daily steps, but must **never author the dream.** Give the needle a wall.
- **One deep goal per direction — a CEILING, not a floor** *(cycle 1, RQ2):* quiet directions are whole, never a deficit. A child's working memory holds one deep goal far better than four shallow ones.

## 2. The halfway point — cut the year in half
The mercy: an unimaginable *year* becomes a reachable *"by winter."* System supplies the **time-anchor**; the learner authors the **content.** *(Proximal subgoals build self-efficacy in children — Bandura-Schunk, cycle 1.)*

> **BULLETPROOF COPY RULE (non-negotiable):** the halfway is **a place to stand, never a due-date.** If a learner doesn't reach it: *"here's where you are"* — **never** *"you missed your halfway."*

## 3. Milestone markers — now → halfway
**2-3 markers**, held **loosely** — a September guess, changeable as you learn; not a contract.

**Plain-language SMART — five questions, drop the acronym:**
1. **What exactly?**
2. **How will you know — what would your buddy or guide see?** *(observable, NOT "hit this number")*
3. **Can you really do it?**
4. **Does it matter to you — and to those you carry?** *(relevant, held as inclusive autonomy)*
5. **By when?**

> **CAUTION:** "measurable" stays *"how will you know?"* (observable evidence), never a metric to hit — the slide from observable → measured is the extraction Kohn warns of. *(cycle 1, RQ4.)*

## 4. Weekly goals — one thing a week
An **invitation** — *"can I do one a week?"* is the learner's own question, never a count the system checks. **No denominator, no "X of Y," no overdue-red.** *(SDT: imposed goals + pressured evaluation diminish intrinsic motivation — cycle 1.)*

## 5. Daily layer — two distinct shapes
- **(a) One-off step** — does a specific rung's work; it *completes.*
- **(b) Standing practice** — a rhythm ("read 30 min"); **no completion, no streak.** Rest is a **first-class choice beside it, never a miss.** *(Streaks trigger shame + abandonment; 80-90% consistency suffices — cycle 1, RQ6.)*

### The practice timer (for time-bound standing practices)
A **learner-started** countdown. **Soft, OPTIONAL** warning + closing chime — never a jarring alarm; a **visual-cue** option for kids who startle. The **learner ends it.** Then a gentle, **skippable** *"Where are you now?"* — that answer becomes the practice's **starting point next time.** Stores **one evolving "now"** — never a session count, minutes total, or streak. A companion that remembers where you were.

## 6. Markers of recognition — the crossings
A reached milestone is **offerable** as a marker — **named by a person** (learner, or witnessing guide), never auto-minted, never for a streak. **Informational + human-named ONLY.** *(cycle 1, RQ5: badges support competence only when informational; harmful as prizes.)*

## 7. The Growth Record seam
**Only human-named recognition seeds the record — never automatic completion.** And *(cycle 1 refinement, RQ6):* the record's **warm Journey view is never subordinated to the professional Portrait export** — the tension between portfolio purposes can undermine motivation; the story stays primary.

## 8. The guide's role — *scaffold and fade* *(resolves the guide bottleneck — cycle 2, RQ6)*
The design does **not** assume infinite guide attention. The guide's model is **scaffold-and-fade**: heavy scaffolding for young / new learners, **faded as readiness grows**, transferring responsibility to the learner. **The fade is the feature** — it is *both* how autonomy develops *and* how the design degrades gracefully at studio scale (one guide, a whole tribe). Match the scaffold to the learner's readiness — some thrive under autonomy, some need explicit guidance.
- The guide **witnesses** (names crossings), **diagnoses before motivating** (a disengaged learner), and **comes alongside** rather than directs.
- **Relatedness is a longitudinal *sustainer*** *(cycle 2, RQ5):* the buddy and the witnessing guide are not decoration — the social layer is what *holds* motivation across a year and beyond.

## 9. Equity — the app carries what the home can't *(cycle 2)*
Lower-SES children *benefit more* from involvement but face barriers (school info rarely reaches them). **Build requirement:** the app and guide must **carry the info-access a low-resource home cannot relay** — never assume the home is the channel. Sovereignty that only works for connected, resourced families is not sovereignty.

## 10. Evidence base (both cycles)
| Design element | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Halfway + milestones | Proximal subgoals build self-efficacy in children — Bandura-Schunk `[T1]` |
| No denominators / rewards / quotas | Rewards undermine intrinsic motivation, *worse for children* — Deci-Koestner-Ryan 128-study meta-analysis `[T1]` |
| Feedback-not-praise | Process-over-person praise, longitudinal — Dweck `[T1]` |
| No-streak practice + rest-is-real | Streak-shame research `[T3, converges w/ T1 SDT]` |
| Record + name-your-crossing | Learner-owned portfolios, learner curates `[T1]` |
| One deep goal (ceiling) | Children's working-memory limits `[T1]` |
| Inclusive autonomy (cultural) | "Autonomy = acting from your values, incl. family"; inclusive autonomy in collectivist groups — Chirkov/Ryan `[T1]` |
| Protect the big goal from parent control | Control on big dreams most harmful `[T1]` |
| Design is trauma-informed | Strengths-based, safety-first, achievable, learner-voice `[T2/T3]` |
| Scaffold-and-fade | Fading transfers responsibility, enables autonomy `[T1]` |
| Relatedness sustains motion | SDT longitudinal — autonomy/competence/relatedness + enjoyment `[T1]` |
| Come-alongside for the disengaged | Motivational interviewing; disengagement masks anxiety/depression `[T2/T3]` |

## 11. The one remaining gap
**Autism-specific goal-setting evidence is thin field-wide** (a research agenda, not settled findings). The *general* EF-scaffolding alignment holds — our proximal-breakdown + off-the-page zoom *is* executive-function support. But **ND-specific accommodations should not rest on evidence we don't have** — flag for a dedicated future cycle or an external autism/ADHD SME before authoring them.

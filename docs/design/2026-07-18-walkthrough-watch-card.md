## For Jenna to sit with: the parent + Spark question

The most supportive role is not the most or least involved. Hold these:

- What can a parent **do with** their Spark to track goals, where the parent supplies the
  **capability** (reading, writing, reminding, celebrating) and the child supplies the
  **content** (the goal, the choice, the pace)?
- The line between helping a child track their own goals and tracking the child. Test:
  **whose goal, in whose words?**
- Home-academics parents: how do we let them reflect that into the child's Compass so the whole
  life is honored as one wheel - without it becoming a report card or a second scorecard?
- Does anything we give the parent ever produce a verdict, rank, or comparison to another child?
  If so, it fails.
- How does the parent's role **taper** as the child grows Spark -> Discovery -> Adventure?

Default mode for a Spark parent = **scaffold / co-pilot** (parent enables, child leads).
Make **driver** mode (parent sets goals and scores) hard or impossible. **Witness** mode is
safe but often too hands-off for a five-year-old who needs a reader.

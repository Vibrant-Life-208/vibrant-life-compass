# PART 1 - THE DECISION GUIDE

*~2 pages. This is what a parent reads to decide.*

## 1. Welcome

Welcome to Vibrant Life.

This guide is short on purpose. It is here to help you make one decision well: whether and how your family steps into the coming year with us, and what that will ask of you.

Here is our promise about everything that follows. These are the things we are committed to, and here is why. You do not have to adopt them. You do have to know them, so your choice is a real one. And we will keep thinking about them together, all year long.

Vibrant Life is an Acton-model studio. Our work rests on three ideas, woven through everything:

- **Learning to Learn** - the skills and habits to teach yourself anything.
- **Learning to Do** - turning curiosity into real work, real projects, real exhibitions.
- **Learning to Be** - the character to use it all well: courage, grit, kindness, honesty.

Our north star is on every page: **Find a calling, change the world.**

**A note on our language.** We use different words on purpose, because the words carry the model:

| Instead of... | We say... |
|---|---|
| Student / Teacher | Learner (or Hero) / Guide |
| Classroom / Classmates | Studio / Tribe mates |
| Grades / Report cards | Mastery, Badges & Exhibitions |
| Worksheets / Textbooks | Quests, mentors & real-life experiences |

## 2. The decision in front of you

You are not signing up for a class schedule. You are choosing a way of learning for your child and a way of participating for your family. This guide walks the decision in the order the questions come:

1. Which studio fits my child this year? *(§3)*
2. What does a day look like, and what are the hours? *(§4)*
3. How does my child grow and move up? *(§5)*
4. What will this ask of me as a parent? *(§6)*

Read those four and you can decide. The Reference Annex (Part 2) is here for once you are in.

## 3. Fields of Play: which studio is my child in?

Learners are grouped by readiness, not strictly by age, so the ages below are a starting point, not a rule (see §5).

| Studio | Ages | In one line |
|---|---|---|
| **Spark** | 5-7 | Curiosity and play growing into self-directed work |
| **Discovery** | 8-10 | Challenges and choices that build an independent learner |
| **Adventure** | 11-14 | Choice plus accountability, turning curiosity into craft |
| **Launchpad** | *(planned)* | Trust your capability; design your next great adventure |
| **Next Great Adventure** | 15-18 | A portfolio, mentors, field hours, and a real Launch Plan |
| **P&T (Parents & Tots)** | youngest | A gentle first taste of the community |

- **Spark (5-7):** Reading (Waseca), early writing, Montessori math, nature and science, and multi-week Quests with little technology. Grows kindness, courage, responsibility, and the first tools for big feelings.
- **Discovery (8-10):** Quests (4-6 weeks) blending science, art, math, and writing into a project with a public exhibition; a writing workshop cycle; deep reading; Khan math; Socratic history. Grows goal-setting, owning results, public speaking, and care for the studio.
- **Adventure (11-14):** Quests (4-8 weeks) in STEM, arts, and entrepreneurship with public exhibitions; primary-source history; science labs; short apprenticeships; badges in the Journey Tracker. Grows servant leadership, feedback, peer accountability, and grit. *(A learner can stay longer if the next studio is not yet open.)*
- **Launchpad (planned):** Mastery shown through drafts, defenses, and real audiences; intentional tech; mentors and real internships. Grows purpose, integrity, and full ownership.
- **Next Great Adventure (15-18):** The capstone. Graduate with a portfolio, mentor references, field hours, and a public Launch Plan - college, trade, venture, service, or gap year.
- **P&T (Parents & Tots):** An introduction-level program; much of this guide will not apply yet. Parent meetings during program time, warm invitations to family activities and Thursday field trips.

## 4. A day at Vibrant Life: the rhythm and the hours

Vibrant Life is **part-time by design** - we power-pack the time we are in studio. That shapes the whole commitment.

| Time | What happens |
|---|---|
| 8:30 - 8:45 am | Outdoor play and drop-off (all weather) |
| 8:45 - 9:00 am | Brain Gym and announcements |
| 9:00 am onward | **Work Block** - the heart of the day (ends late morning for younger studios, early afternoon for older) |
| Midday | Lunch and outdoor play, eaten outside; studios closed except the bathroom |
| ~1:50 - 2:00 pm | Pickup; the day closes at 2:00 pm |

**On attendance** - this shapes the commitment:

> "With VL being part-time we really power-pack the time when we are in studio. If you miss days, it's life, we get it. We don't keep attendance. When a learner is out it's their responsibility to check in and make a plan."

So the commitment is less about perfect attendance and more about showing up ready to use the time. Plan trips for break weeks where you can; a quick BAND message covers an absence.

**Weather:** assume we are in session, rain or snow; a BAND message goes out by 7:30 am if anything changes. You are the best judge of safety for your family.

## 5. How a learner grows and moves up

At Vibrant Life, **learners advance by readiness, not by birthdays.** A learner moves up when clear thresholds are met - self-governance, completed goals, demonstrated mastery - and has genuinely grown. By May, an age-eligible learner prepares a **Pitch to the Next Studio** with evidence of growth. If thresholds are not yet met, the path simply continues until readiness comes. The question is always *are they ready,* never *how old are they.*

**How growth is tracked.** Instead of grades, learners earn **badges**: mastery credentials backed by real, publishable work, not prizes for finishing. Each learner has an annual badge plan, co-created with you and the guide, visible in the online **Journey Tracker**. Learning is **self-paced** - the only comparison is you, yesterday. Mastery can take more than a year, because a solid foundation matters more than speed.

**How learners get unstuck: "3 B's before G."** Before asking a Guide, a learner tries their own **Brains**, the available **Books** and resources, and a **Buddy**. Only then do they ask a guide, who answers with leading questions rather than the answer. It builds the self-reliance the whole model rests on, and you can reinforce it at home.

## 6. What Vibrant Life asks of you as a parent

Choosing Vibrant Life is a family commitment, not a drop-off. Plainly, here is what we ask:

- **Be reachable during the day** for a bathroom need or a moment of extra support, especially for younger learners.
- **Join the community** - a volunteer committee and the Skills Form, so we know what you would love to offer.
- **Come to parent meetings** - quarterly, on a topic of growth; we ask for at least three of the four.
- **Show up for the annual Journey Meeting** - you, your learner, and the guide set goals and celebrate growth. Ideally both parents.
- **Partner, do not rescue** - coach rather than fix. Curious questions over quick answers. Your calm is contagious.
- **Bring an all-weather mindset** - much of the day is outside, on purpose, in every season.
- **Provide the gear and the device** - all-weather layers (thrift is great), plus a labeled Chromebook for Discovery and Adventure and home computer access for core work.
- **Tend yourself, too** - take care of yourself and lean on the community (Book Nooks, Mom Meet-Ups, Dad Campfires).

**How we will communicate.** BAND for absences, quick questions, and emergencies; email the guides (vibrantlife208guides@gmail.com) for meetings and longer questions; a meeting with the owner for anything sensitive. Expect a reply within three days during the session.

**If it is ever not the right fit.** We resolve issues through conversation first - we ask for two meetings with the guide and two with Jenna before withdrawing. If a transition is still right, the current month's tuition is prorated and refunded. We part ways, when we must, with grace and mutual respect.

## How you will see progress
- **Exhibitions of Learning** - "living report cards." Each session, learners showcase their work; guides do not step in, because the exhibition is the learning. Families invited.
- **Character call-outs** - we name the courage, kindness, grit, and honesty we see in each other.
- **The Journey Tracker** - your window into the badge plan, weekly progress, and goals.

## Everyday rhythms
- **Food & water:** nourishing food and a labeled water bottle daily; lighter on sugar, not "never"; different dietary philosophies respected.
- **Nature & play:** central at every age, with room for managed risk - climbing, bare feet, muddy boots. We let learners assess their own risk; you hear from us about anything serious, not every boo-boo.
- **Technology as a tool, not a toy:** no social media or games in studio; safety filters; P&T screen-free; Discovery and Adventure sign a tech agreement.
- **Health & illness:** keep your child home for the symptoms in the onboarding guide; our stance trusts each family's judgment.
- **A simple environment:** toys and books stay home (except show-and-tell); shoes-off welcome inside.
- **What to bring:** labeled water bottle, lunch and snack, backpack, weather layers.

## Tools we use to help learners self-regulate
Each morning and as needed: **Brain Gym**, **Heart Coherence** breathing, the **Wheel of Awareness**, **4-7-8 breathing**, tapping (EFT), and calming aromatherapy. When learners can influence their own nervous system, life becomes a series of choices rather than reactions.

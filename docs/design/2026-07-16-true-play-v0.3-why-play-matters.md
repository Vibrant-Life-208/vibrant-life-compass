## The reframe: from "free" to *why play matters*

v0.2 opened with a gift-frame ("handed to you free, day one"). That answers "what
does this cost me?" - but the question a tired parent carries is "why should this
matter when I'm already drowning?" Lead with the stakes; let the "free" quality be
something the parent *feels* (nothing is asked back, nothing is counted), not
something we announce.

**Candidate new entry copy** (permission-and-relief register):

> Play is not the reward for a childhood well-managed. It is the thing childhood is for.
> A child who is held, who can rest in your togetherness, has energy left over - and
> that leftover energy becomes play. Play is where they try on courage, metabolize what
> scared them, and find out who they are when nothing is for a grade. The research is
> blunt about the other side: children robbed of play grow more anxious, more rigid,
> less able to tell friend from foe. This isn't enrichment. It's a nutrient.
> And it doesn't stop at childhood. The parent who became all-function, who can't
> remember the last time their own emotions came out to play - that's not a character
> flaw, it's play-starvation in an adult. You can't hand a child a world you've exiled
> yourself from.

**Hard caution (SDT / condition C1):** "play is SO important" can become one more
thing an exhausted parent is *failing at* - the exact shame the soft-landing exists
to prevent. If we deepen the stakes, we deepen the landing by the same amount, every
time. Re-run the SDT test against THIS copy specifically (Research thread 4).

## The four beats, expanded

Each beat: meaning · why to parenting · why to a life · candidate talk.
Every video below is a SEARCH CANDIDATE, not vetted. Watching in full is a hard
step (Research thread 3), not a nicety.

### Beat 1 - Right relationships · "You are the answer, not the one with the answers."
- **Meaning:** Attachment is the soil, not the fertilizer. A child explores *because*
  the tie to you is secure enough to leave from.
- **Parenting:** Takes the pressure off having answers. Your presence is the intervention.
- **A life:** Every act of courage launches from some experience of secure return.
- **Candidate:** Molly Wright, *How every child can thrive by five* (TED, ~7.5 min,
  serve-and-return, live peek-a-boo). Or the Tronick Still Face clip (~3 min) as the
  visceral version of the answer going still.

### Beat 2 - Soft hearts · "Tears they can cry are tears that don't have to become armor."
- **Meaning:** A held child stays emotionally tender - feelings move through rather
  than calcify into defendedness.
- **Parenting:** In the hard moment, be safe enough that the feeling can move; repair,
  not perfection.
- **A life:** Tronick: even secure pairs fall out of sync ~70% of the time; what holds
  the bond is repair after rupture, not its absence.
- **Candidate:** Dr. Becky Kennedy on repair ("a 15-second intervention that can change
  the trajectory of a child's life"). Pairs with the Still Face rupture-repair frame.

### Beat 3 - Rest · "Rest is where growth happens."
- **Meaning:** Neufeld's "activated rest" - the striving agendas go quiet because
  nothing is for real; the brain does repair/memory/growth work at rest.
- **Parenting:** You don't manufacture growth by pushing; you make room for rest.
- **A life:** The anti-hustle nutrient. Creativity and healing happen in the trough.
- **Candidate:** Neufeld himself on the essential nature of play/rest (Family 360,
  Eps. 5-6, 32 - AUDIO, and the primary source; the beat where the source speaks).

### Beat 4 - True play · "The bubble where nothing counts."
- **Meaning:** Not toys, not games, not "educational play." Because it doesn't count,
  the child can risk, invent, become.
- **Parenting:** "You don't have to build it. You just have to not get in its way."
  Subtraction, not addition.
- **A life:** Play is a primary emotional system (Panksepp), alongside fear and care.
  Brown's play-deprivation research (Texas tower shooter → incarcerated violent
  offenders) links sustained early play-deprivation to emotional dysregulation,
  rigidity, poor impulse control, fragile relationships.
- **Candidates:** Stuart Brown, *Play is more than just fun* (TED); Peter Gray,
  *The Decline of Play* (TEDxNavesink, ~16 min). Both longer than a threshold moment
  wants → "go deeper" links, not in-ritual media.

## What the media looks like (+ the sharp catch)

- One optional short per beat. Never autoplay, never required, never "watch all 4 to
  complete" (that rebuilds the ladder we tore down).
- In-ritual media stays tiny (3-min Still Face, 7-min Molly Wright). The 16-26 min
  canonical talks (Brown, Gray) live behind a quiet "if you want to go deeper" door.
- Captioned, low-bandwidth, sound-off by default (matches Canor's silent-by-default wall).

**CATCH (dissent decision #4, sharpened):** the source says screens kill play, and we
would answer him by adding more screen. A faithful True Play feature might, at its most
honest, say "close the app and go find your kid." Before any video ships, answer
cleanly: *does this media pull a parent toward their child, or toward the phone?* If we
can't answer that, the videos betray the thesis.

## Making the learning playful (kids' surfaces)

Learning-sciences consensus (Zosh et al. 2017, LEGO Foundation): learning-through-play
is **joyful, meaningful, actively engaging, iterative, socially interactive.**

Key: playful learning is the OPPOSITE of gamification. Gamification bolts
points/streaks/badges onto learning (extraction dressed as fun). Playful learning makes
the *learning itself* carry the five qualities - no external reward. Fits the existing
SDT wall on learner surfaces (studios.js weeding of Khan streaks / book-counts into
self-set aims). The parent-badge philosophy and the learner philosophy become ONE
philosophy: rest and play over proving and counting, on both sides of the family.

Sketch (provisional; curriculum text Jenna-gated):
- Audit learner surfaces against the five characteristics (joyful vs dutiful, iterative
  vs one-shot, meaningful vs arbitrary).
- Reframe "goals" as invitations/experiments (iterative), not targets.
- Protect a genuinely un-scored "nothing counts here" zone - the child's version of the
  parent's threshold door.
- Keep the SDT wall exactly where it is. Playful != more reward machinery.

## Research to catch issues before a family sees it and before audit

Ordered by leverage:
1. **Parent listening study (highest; unblocks Step 1).** See protocol doc. The thing
   the design has never had. Everything else is secondary.
2. **Primary-source fidelity (owed since v0.2).** Verify four beats + six stages against
   *Hold On to Your Kids* (Neufeld & Maté) + Neufeld Institute course material - not the
   transcript, not a WebFetch summary (a summary isn't citation-grade).
3. **Video vetting - watch every candidate fully.** Length, tone, licensing/embedding,
   captions, and the screens-contradict-play test. None embeds until watched whole.
4. **SDT re-check on the new *why* framing.** Deepened stakes raise C1 risk (token
   becoming expected; play becoming a performance a parent can fail). Test the NEW copy.
5. **Cultural-universality check (Stirps).** Doorway archetype + attachment-parenting
   norms are culturally situated. Don't encode one culture's ideal parent as universal.
6. **Accessibility (Chekov).** Captions, sound-off default, low-bandwidth, no autoplay,
   reduced-motion honored on the door animation.

Headline: **items 1-3 catch the issues that matter; 4-6 make it safe; item 1 tells us
whether to build it at all.**

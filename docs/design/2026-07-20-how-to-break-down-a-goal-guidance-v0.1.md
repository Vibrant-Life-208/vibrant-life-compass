# How to Break Down a Goal — guidance v0.1

**Date:** 2026-07-20. **Status:** v0.1 — resolvable problems baked in; six ecosystem risks flagged for research cycle 2. **NOT final.**
**Origin:** fleet goal-breakdown meeting (Ezri, Comes, Accord, Jake, Bareil, Polaris, Data, Lux) + PDC research cycle (`agents/governance/research-briefs/2026-07-20-pdc-research-brief.md`).
**Scope of v0.1:** the *framework, principles, and plain-language SMART* — with the evidence base. The per-age **learner-facing** and **guide-facing** copy is the next authoring pass (v1, after cycle 2).
**Honest frame (Polaris/Gnosis):** cycle 1 validated the *mechanism*; it did not test the *ecosystem* (guide, parent, equity, culture, the long arc). This guidance is for the child the research has met — see §9 for the child it hasn't.

## 0. The fork at the top — before any breakdown

**Every goal is first *doing* or *becoming*.** This decides everything below.
- **Doing (finish-shaped)** — the ladder in §1-7 applies.
- **Becoming (presence — Heart / Spirit / Voice / the Grows side of any region)** — **none** of the ladder. No halfway, no milestones, no weekly quota, no completion badge. Presence only: the destination held, the *noticing* below it, and **nothing sequence-shaped between them.** (The ratified becoming carve-out; per-goal track flag.)
- **"Not right now" is a real answer.** A learner may decline or pause a goal this season and be **whole** — no goal is required in any direction. (Ported from the Kitchen Table's *present mode*: the companion stays even when there's no goal to walk.)

## 1. The destination — the year goal
From the year vision — *"who you're becoming / what's different in a year."* **One deep goal per direction as a CEILING, not a floor.** Quiet directions are whole, never a deficit. *(Children's working-memory limits: one deep goal beats a multi-goal spread — RQ2, T1.)*

## 2. The halfway point — cut the year in half
The mercy: an unimaginable *year* becomes a reachable *"by winter."* The system supplies the **time-anchor** (the midpoint); the learner authors the **content** (what they'll have reached). *(Proximal subgoals build self-efficacy in children — Bandura & Schunk 1981, T1.)*

> **BULLETPROOF COPY RULE (non-negotiable):** the halfway is **a place to stand, never a due-date.** If a learner doesn't reach it, the surface reads *"here's where you are"* — **never** *"you missed your halfway."* One weak sentence here leaks the whole no-behind architecture.

## 3. Milestone markers — now → halfway
**2-3 markers**, held **loosely** — your best guess in September, changeable as you learn. Not a contract; a learner may reach the halfway by a different road, and that's *growth*, not failure.

**Plain-language SMART — the five questions, drop the acronym** *(Jake: "SMART" is adult/compliance jargon):*
1. **What exactly?** *(specific)*
2. **How will you know — what would your buddy or guide see?** *(measurable — as **observable**, NOT "hit this number")*
3. **Can you really do it?** *(achievable)*
4. **Does it matter to *you*?** *(relevant — protects the learner's own want)*
5. **By when?** *(time-bound)*

> **CAUTION (knife's edge):** "measurable" must stay *"how will you know?"* (observable evidence), never a metric to hit. The slide from observable → measured is the exact extraction Kohn warns of. *(RQ4, T1.)*

## 4. Weekly goals — one thing a week
An **invitation**, not a quota: *"can I do one a week?"* is a question the **learner asks themselves**, never a count the system checks. **No denominator, no "X of Y," no overdue-red.** *(SDT: imposed goals + pressured evaluation diminish intrinsic motivation — RQ4, T1.)*

## 5. Daily layer — two distinct shapes
- **(a) One-off step** — does a specific rung's work; it *completes.*
- **(b) Standing practice** — a rhythm ("read 30 min"); **no completion, no streak.** Rest is a **first-class choice beside it, never a miss.** *(Streaks trigger shame + all-or-nothing abandonment; 80-90% consistency suffices — RQ6.)*

### The practice timer (for time-bound standing practices)
A **learner-started** countdown for "work on ___ for 30 min." **Soft, OPTIONAL** warning + closing chime — never a jarring alarm; a **visual-cue** option for kids who startle. The **learner ends it.** Then a gentle, **skippable** *"Where are you now?"* — and that answer becomes the practice's **starting point next time.** Stores **one evolving "now"** — never a session count, minutes total, or streak. A companion that remembers where you were, not a tracker that scores what you did.

## 6. Markers of recognition — the crossings
A reached milestone is **offerable** as a marker of recognition — **named by a person** (the learner, or a witnessing guide), *"you reached this, and it mattered."* **Informational + human-named ONLY** — never auto-minted, never for a streak or frequency. *(RQ5, T1: badges support competence only when informational; harmful as prizes.)*

## 7. The Growth Record seam
**Only human-named recognition seeds the record — never automatic completion.** This keeps the transcript a witnessed *story*, not a compliance *log*. *(Learner-owned portfolios strengthen self-concept, especially when the learner chooses what to keep — RQ6.)*

## 8. Evidence base (why this works — from the 2026-07-20 PDC cycle)
- **Halfway + milestones** ← proximal subgoals build self-efficacy in children (Bandura-Schunk, T1).
- **No denominators / rewards / quotas** ← rewards undermine intrinsic motivation, *worse for children*, worst on already-loved tasks (Deci-Koestner-Ryan 128-study meta-analysis, T1). *The Category Wall is the mechanism, not the nicety.*
- **Feedback-not-praise** ← process-over-person praise, longitudinal (Dweck, T1).
- **No-streak practice + rest-is-real** ← streak-shame research (RQ6).
- **Record + name-your-crossing** ← learner-owned portfolios, learner curates (RQ6, T1).
- **One deep goal (ceiling)** ← children's working-memory limits (RQ2, T1).

## 9. What this guidance does NOT yet account for — v0.1 open risks (→ research cycle 2, SSC-weighted)
*The mechanism is validated; the ecosystem is not. These are honest gaps, not oversights.*
1. **Guide capacity** — the design assumes a present, skilled guide witnessing every learner. At studio scale (one guide, a whole tribe) this may not exist. Does the design **degrade gracefully** when the guide is stretched?
2. **Parent dynamics** — nothing here protects the learner's **needle** from parent imposition, which RQ4 says harms child motivation most. How is learner autonomy protected in a parent-involved system?
3. **Equity / neurodivergence / trauma** — untested for learners with limited home support, ADHD/autism, or trauma histories. Does "quiet is whole" help, or get read as deficit?
4. **Longitudinal motivation** — the evidence is largely short-term; the Compass is a *year*, and the record a *childhood.* What sustains motivation across months and developmental stages?
5. **Cultural frames (Kira)** — *"author your own life / your needle / what do YOU want"* is a Western individualist frame. Untested across collectivist family contexts, where selfhood is woven into community. **Whose model of a self does this center?**
6. **The disengaged learner** — the "not right now" door is designed, but the **re-engagement path** (a learner in a hard season, or resistant) is not.

→ These six are the research questions for **cycle 2**. v1 of this guidance follows that cycle.

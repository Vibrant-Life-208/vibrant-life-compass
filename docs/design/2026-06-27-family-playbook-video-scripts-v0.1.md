# Vibrant Life Playbook - Video Scripts v0.1

> Four videos for the 2026-2027 Family Guide, cut from `...-condensed-v0.3.md`. Format per beat: **VISUAL** (what's on screen) / **VOICE** (spoken) / **CAPTION** (on-screen text, for sound-off viewing). Each script ends with its two Kohlberg-L6 gate checks.
>
> **Production stance (from the meeting):** keep it real, not slick. Phone footage of actual studios and learners beats a polished promo. Fabula's bar: *"she pictures her child here," not "what a nice video."* Caption everything (many parents watch muted). Ceiling is four videos - resist adding more.

## VIDEO 1 - "Welcome to Vibrant Life"

**Runtime ~85s | On camera/voice: Jenna (warmest as the owner) | Footage: arrivals, outdoor play, a studio mid-work | Opens on the mission.**

**[0:00-0:14]**
- **VISUAL:** Morning. Learners arriving, outdoor play, a guide greeting a child by name.
- **VOICE:** "Welcome to Vibrant Life. Our mission is simple: to inspire every person who walks through our doors to find a calling and change the world."
- **CAPTION:** *To find a calling and change the world.*

**[0:14-0:28]**
- **VISUAL:** A child fully absorbed - reading, building - a quiet, proud moment.
- **VOICE:** "We believe every child is born with a gift, something bright and completely their own. This journey isn't just about learning. It's about becoming."
- **CAPTION:** *Every child is born with a gift.*

**[0:28-0:42]**
- **VISUAL:** Jenna to camera, warm, unhurried.
- **VOICE:** "Here's our promise about everything in our playbook. These are the things we're committed to, and why. You don't have to adopt them. You only have to know them, so your choice is a real one."
- **CAPTION:** *You don't have to adopt these. You have to know them.*

**[0:42-0:56]**
- **VISUAL:** Quick real moments: a Quest project; kids resolving something outside.
- **VOICE:** "Everything rests on three ideas: learning to learn, learning to do, and learning to be."
- **CAPTION:** *Learning to Learn · to Do · to Be*

**[0:56-1:10]**
- **VISUAL:** A guide kneeling beside a learner, asking a question rather than giving an answer.
- **VOICE:** "We use different words on purpose. Not students - learners, heroes of their own journey. Not teachers - guides. Not grades - mastery, shown in real work."
- **CAPTION:** *Learners & Guides · Mastery, not grades*

**[1:10-1:22]**
- **VISUAL:** Wide shot of the grounds; tagline resolves.
- **VOICE:** "Find a calling, change the world. Take your time with our playbook. We'll think about it together."
- **CAPTION:** *Find a calling, change the world.*

> **L6 gate - poses or deposits?** Poses: opens on the mission and the promise, then invites the parent to decide ("take your time... we'll think about it together"). No "enroll now."
> **L6 gate - valued or used?** Valued: leads with the child's gift and becoming, not with a sales hook; warmth serves understanding, not pressure.

## VIDEO 2 - "A Day at Vibrant Life"

**Runtime ~90s | Voice: light VO over footage | Re-aimed (per 2026-06-29 review): the day now lands the growth-without-grades story - the question parents most want answered.**

**[0:00-0:10]**
- **VISUAL:** 8:30 am, kids spilling into outdoor play, all weather.
- **VOICE:** "A day here has a rhythm. Let us walk you through it - and show you how a learner grows."
- **CAPTION:** *A day at Vibrant Life*

**[0:10-0:24]**
- **VISUAL:** Brain Gym on the grass; cross-lateral movement; "Good morning!" / "Yes it is!"
- **VOICE:** "We start outside and in our bodies - movement, a greeting, a few minutes to settle and connect."
- **CAPTION:** *8:45 - Brain Gym*

**[0:24-0:44]**
- **VISUAL:** The Work Block: morning circle, a Socratic launch, Quest project work, reading, a small group problem-solving.
- **VOICE:** "Then the work block, the heart of the day - circle, Quests, reading and math, real projects, and learners figuring things out together."
- **CAPTION:** *9:00 - the Work Block*

**[0:44-1:06] - the growth beat (the heart of this video)**
- **VISUAL:** A learner presenting at an exhibition; a badge earned; a parent watching, proud; a learner's own goal chart.
- **VOICE:** "You won't see grades or report cards here. Instead, learners earn badges for real, mastered work, and show what they've learned at exhibitions you're invited to. Some move quickly, some take longer - and the only person a learner races is who they were yesterday."
- **CAPTION:** *No grades. Badges, exhibitions, and real growth.*

**[1:06-1:20]**
- **VISUAL:** Lunch outside, extended play, climbing, muddy boots.
- **VOICE:** "Lunch and play happen outside, rain or shine. Outside is where a lot of the learning lives, too."
- **CAPTION:** *Midday - outside, all weather*

**[1:20-1:32]**
- **VISUAL:** Pickup at the roundabout; a learner walking out tired and proud.
- **VOICE:** "We're part-time by design - we power-pack our time together. Two o'clock comes, and they leave a little more themselves."
- **CAPTION:** *Part-time by design · ends 2:00 pm*

> **L6 gate - poses or deposits?** Poses: shows *how* growth works (badges, exhibitions, readiness) and lets the parent judge the fit - it answers the real question instead of dodging it.
> **L6 gate - valued or used?** Valued: honest footage (weather, mess, effort, a real exhibition), not a glossy highlight reel; "the only comparison is you, yesterday" centers the child, not a ranking.

## VIDEO 3 - Studio Shorts (one per studio, 30-45s each)

**Voice: that studio's guide, on camera | Footage: that studio mid-work | Each short stands alone, so a family with learners in more than one studio can watch each of their children's.**

**Reusable shape:**
1. Guide intro (name + studio).
2. "Generally ages [x]."
3. *What they do* (one line) + *who they become* (one line), from §3.
4. A posing close: "If your child is ready for [the belief], this might be their studio."

---

*Studio set & colors (per captain, youngest-first): **Tots = orange, Spark = yellow, Discovery = purple, Adventure = green, Launch Pad (coming soon) = blue.** Next Great Adventure is folded into Launch Pad. Tint each short's title card / lower-third in its studio color.*

### Tots Tribe (Parents & Tots · ages 3-4) - ORANGE - guide on camera
- **VOICE:** "I'm [guide], and I welcome our youngest in the Tots Tribe, generally ages three to four. It's a gentle first taste of the Vibrant Life community, where play and experience are the important work of the child. You'll have parent meetings during program time, plus invitations to family activities and field trips. If you'd love a warm first step into this community with your little one, the Tots Tribe is for you."
- **CAPTION:** *Tots Tribe · ages 3-4 · a gentle first taste*

### Spark (ages 5-7) - YELLOW - guide on camera
- **VOICE:** "Hi, I'm [guide], and I guide Spark, generally for ages five to seven. Here, curiosity and purposeful play grow into self-directed work - early reading and writing, Montessori math, nature and science, and Quests with very little technology. Along the way they grow kindness, courage, and the first tools for big feelings. If your child is ready to learn through play, Spark may be their studio."
- **CAPTION:** *Spark · ages 5-7 · play into self-directed work*

### Discovery (ages 8-10) - PURPLE - guide on camera
- **VOICE:** "I'm [guide], and I guide Discovery, generally ages eight to ten. Learners take on Quests that blend science, art, math, and writing into a real project with a public exhibition. They draft and revise their writing, read deeply, and join Socratic discussions, growing into someone who sets goals and owns results. If your child is ready for purposeful challenges, Discovery may be their studio."
- **CAPTION:** *Discovery · ages 8-10 · the independent learner*

### Adventure (ages 11-14) - GREEN - guide on camera
- **VOICE:** "I'm [guide], guide for Adventure, generally ages eleven to fourteen. Adventure turns curiosity into a craft - longer Quests in STEM, the arts, and entrepreneurship, primary-source history, science labs, short apprenticeships, and badges that track real mastery. They take on servant-leadership, give and receive honest feedback, and build grit. If your child is ready for choice with real accountability, Adventure may be their studio."
- **CAPTION:** *Adventure · ages 11-14 · curiosity into craft*

### Launch Pad (ages 15-18, coming soon) - BLUE - title card + voiceover
- **VOICE:** "Launch Pad is on its way - generally ages fifteen to eighteen. It's where a learner comes to know who they are, trust their capability, and design their next great adventure: mastery through real audiences, mentors and internships, graduating with a portfolio and a public Launch Plan - college, trade, venture, service, or a gap year. We'll share more as it opens."
- **CAPTION (full screen):** *Launch Pad · ages 15-18 · coming soon*

> **L6 gate - poses or deposits?** Poses: each closes with "if your child is ready for X, this may be their studio" - the parent decides the fit.
> **L6 gate - valued or used?** Valued: a guide's real face and voice; describes the work honestly, no superlatives or competition framing.

## VIDEO 4 - "What We Ask of You"

**Runtime ~60s | On camera: Jenna, direct and warm | Footage: parents volunteering, a Journey Meeting, outdoor pickup**

**[0:00-0:12]**
- **VISUAL:** Jenna to camera.
- **VOICE:** "Vibrant Life asks real things of a family. We want you to know them up front, so you can decide if this fits your season."
- **CAPTION:** *What we ask of you*

**[0:12-0:40]**
- **VISUAL:** Quick honest cutaways matching each ask - a parent on a committee, a quarterly meeting, an all-weather pickup, a Chromebook in a backpack.
- **VOICE:** "We ask you to be reachable during the day. To join the community and volunteer your gifts. To come to at least three of our four parent meetings, and to your child's yearly Journey Meeting. To partner rather than rescue when your child struggles. To bring an all-weather mindset. And to provide outdoor layers, plus a Chromebook for our older studios."
- **CAPTION:** *Reachable · Volunteer · Parent meetings · Partner, don't rescue · All weather · Gear*

**[0:40-1:00]**
- **VISUAL:** A learner working something out independently; a quiet proud moment.
- **VOICE:** "That is a real commitment. For the families who choose it, it is how a child grows into someone who can govern themselves. Take a look at what we ask, and see if it's the partnership you want."
- **CAPTION:** *See if it's the partnership you want.*

> **L6 gate - poses or deposits?** Poses: explicitly frames the commitment as something the parent weighs and decides ("see if it's the partnership you want").
> **L6 gate - valued or used?** Valued: the honest cost is named in full and not softened; no guilt, no "good parents do this" framing.

## Our Mission
**To inspire each person who enters our doors to find a calling and change the world.**

Our mission is two-fold:
1. **We empower families to grow - and grow together.** Foundations and attitudes toward education happen first, and most powerfully, in the family.
2. **We prepare learners to hear a call to adventure, begin a Hero's Journey, and ultimately find a calling and change the world.**

At Vibrant Life, we believe every child is born with a gift - something beautiful, bright, and completely their own. Our mission is to create a space where that gift is seen, nurtured, and called forward. *This journey isn't just about learning. It's about becoming.*

## Welcome to the Playbook  ‹L6 A1›

This playbook isn't a rulebook. It's a guide - a shared understanding of how we move through this journey together as a community of families, learners, and guides. It will keep evolving as we change.

*Here is our promise about everything that follows. These are the things we are committed to, and here is why. You do not have to adopt them. You do have to know them, so your choice is a real one. And we will keep thinking about them together, all year.*

We created it for three reasons: **to help you feel grounded** (a place to find answers when questions arise), **to build clarity and trust** (clear agreements keep us focused on growth, not confusion), and **to honor the heart of what we're building** (a space that lifts the learner and the family).

---

## What We Stand For
1. **Learners are whole people.** They arrive with creativity, emotions, curiosity, and voice. They are geniuses. We honor both heart and mind.
2. **Families are partners and the primary educators.** Parents hold the primary responsibility for their child's education; we walk beside you and support it. When family and VL share the vision, the learner thrives.
3. **The learning environment matters.** We design inspiring spaces where learners feel safe, challenged, and have a voice - intentional, not perfect.
4. **Purpose leads the way.** We're not here to "do school." We help learners discover who they are and what they're called to bring to the world.

**What we hold, and what's yours.** Because families are the primary educators, we keep our focus on academics, character, emotional development, and community. In studio we don't offer instruction on charged political topics, human sexuality, or gender identity - families guide those conversations at home. We hold deep respect for each family's economic, political, and religious freedom, and we help young people learn *how* to think, not *what* to think.

**How to use this book:** Read it. Use it. Revisit it. If you ask a question we've answered here, we'll lovingly guide you back to it - not because we don't care, but because we do. That's the learner-led way.

## Philosophy & Program at a Glance
- **Program model:** an **accredited Acton Academy** running as a **part-time comprehensive program with a wellness niche.** Parents don't need to purchase additional curriculum or teach at home - your membership includes the materials and curriculum your child needs. In the older studios, some work is done at home; those programs are self-directed with built-in instruction.
- **How learning looks:** traditional schools divide the day into long subject blocks. We do focused core work to master foundations *and* lean into real-world learning where subjects blend through projects, play, and community - so core skills surface throughout the day in authentic contexts.
- **A health note:** the tools in this playbook are not medical advice - please consult licensed professionals as needed.

## Learning to Learn, Do & Be
- **Learning to Learn (processes):** finding the recipes, processes, tools, and habits of critical thinking that help us make better decisions.
- **Learning to Do (skills + academics + real world):** making practical decisions and taking real-world action to accomplish great tasks.
- **Learning to Be (character + matters of the heart):** the most important part of the Hero's Journey is not the Grail but how the hero is changed in the process. We help each learner meet hard moments with courage, build durable habits of character, and transform energy-draining habits into practices that support their highest self.

> *"We delight in the beauty of the butterfly but rarely admit the changes it has gone through to achieve that beauty."* - Maya Angelou

Three questions we hope each learner can answer at the end of a full life: *Did I contribute something meaningful? Did I actively create the life I wanted? Did I give and receive love to the best of my ability?*

## The Rules: Why They're So Clear
The learner-led way is best thought of as **a game.** Imagine you're playing soccer, and someone starts using their hands. Are you still playing soccer? Maybe it's a fun game - but it isn't soccer anymore. Vibrant Life is **different by design.** By clearly outlining how we play together, we can serve your family well and deliver on our promises.

**Vibrant Life is a team effort** - parents, learners, guides, and owners all uphold the mission. **Guardrails** define what's in and out of bounds; guides and Jenna hold them so learners can struggle, grow, and thrive:
1. **Hold Studio Sacred**
2. **Be Kind & Respectful** (physical and emotional safety)
3. **Minimize Distraction** (to ensure intentional growth)

**Studio Commitments** each learner agrees to (held until the studio writes its own Session-1 contract): *integrity, respect & communication, excellence, inner work, community,* and *responsibility & preparation.*

## The Players: Parents, Learners & Guides
We name each player's role - not to promote rule-following, but to create the clarity that frees everyone to innovate together.

> *"If you change the way you look at things, the things you look at change."* - Wayne Dyer

**As parents, we promise to** (from the Parent Agreement of Participation): be on our own Hero's Journey; accept a self-paced environment where learners run the studios; help our learner set goals and meet with them and their guide at **Journey Meetings** (twice a year, around Sessions 2 and 5); let our learner fail early, cheaply, and often; attend the majority of Exhibitions and quarterly Parent Meetings (ideally both parents); extend grace; use Open Forums and nonviolent communication; and **stay out of the Drama Triangle (victim, persecutor, rescuer) and operate in the Empowerment Triangle (creator, challenger, coach)** - see *The Power of TED* by David Emerald.

**A Guide is** a non-judgmental challenger, cheerleader, game-maker, asker of Socratic questions, and equipper with tools, who lives their own Hero's Journey. **A guide promises you to:**
- see your child as a genius with a gift to offer the world;
- turn responsibility over to the learners as quickly as possible;
- hold the studio guardrails that keep everyone physically and emotionally safe;
- answer with Socratic questions rather than answers, so the accomplishment stays the learner's;
- celebrate effort, courage, and progress, and offer growth-minded support when the path is hard;
- be on their own Hero's Journey, evolving right alongside your child, and keep growing through 360 feedback.

## Fields of Play: Our Studios  ‹NEW 26-27›
*Mixed-age studios with a shared vision. Mastery starts with the basics - like John Wooden teaching his champions to tie their shoes. Learners are grouped by readiness, not strictly by age. From the foundational Tots Tribe up, each studio becomes increasingly learner-led.*

**Tots Tribe** *(Parents & Tots · generally ages 3-4)* `‹NEW 26-27›`

- *A day:* welcome circle, movement, nature invitation, relationship and practical-life skills, art, story, snack, tidy, goodbye.

**Spark** *(generally ages 5-7)*
- *Guiding Belief:* curiosity and purposeful play growing into self-directed work.
- *Learning to Be:* kindness, courage, personal responsibility; being a good friend; collaboration; emotional regulation tools.
- *Learning to Learn & Do:* strong reading (Waseca), handwriting and early writing, Montessori math, nature and science, multi-week Quests with little to no technology, and exhibitions.

**Discovery** *(generally ages 8-10)*
- *Guiding Belief:* purposeful challenges + choices grow independent learners and a strong sense of self.

- *Learning to Learn & Do:* Quests (4-6 week projects with public exhibition), a writing workshop cycle, deep reading and DEAR, online Khan math, story-rich Civilization with Socratic dialogue, and a respectful relationship with technology via the Journey Tracker.

**Adventure** *(generally ages 11-14)*
- *Guiding Belief:* choice + accountability turn curiosity into a disciplined craft.
- *Learning to Be:* servant leadership, feedback, peer accountability to contracts, creator mindset, reflective journaling and public speaking, grit.
- *Learning to Learn & Do:* Quests (4-8 week STEM/arts/entrepreneurship with public exhibitions), writing workshops, primary-source Civilization, science labs, apprenticeships and service, badges in the Journey Tracker. *(A learner can stay in Adventure longer if the Launch Pad studio is not yet established. Valuable learning happens either way.)*

**Launch Pad** *(generally ages 15-18 · coming soon)* `‹NEW 26-27›`
- *Guiding Belief:* know who you are, trust your capability, and design your next great adventure.
- *Learning to Be:* purpose and servant leadership; high-trust integrity; habits of excellence; own your results and impact.
- *Learning to Learn & Do:* mastery shown through drafts, defenses, and real audiences; intentional tech; mentors and real internships; graduate with a portfolio, mentor references, field hours, and a public **Launch Plan** (college, trade, venture, service, or gap year). *Coming soon - we'll share more as it opens.*

## Food, Birthdays & Celebrations
Nourishing food and a labeled water bottle daily; we go lighter on sugar (not "never"), and respect different dietary philosophies. Birthdays are marked with a **Circle of Appreciation** - the birthday child hears one sincere, character-based compliment from each peer and shares one thing they appreciate about themselves - plus a peacock feather for their unique beauty.

## Outside Play & Exhibitions
Outdoor time and real play are central, with room for managed risk (tree climbing, bare feet, muddy boots) and an Outside Play Contract with restorative steps. **Exhibitions of Learning** are living report cards: at each session's end, learners showcase their work; guides don't step in, because the exhibition is the learning. Open to parents, grandparents, and friends.

> *"Encourage your child to have muddy, grassy, or sandy feet by the end of each day - that's the childhood they deserve."* - Penny Whitehouse
> *"I took a walk in the woods and came out taller than the trees."* - Henry David Thoreau

## Technology + More
Computers are for learning and creation - studio time is social-media- and game-free, with safety filters. The aim is to coach learners to make good choices ("tools, not toys"). Tots are screen-free; Spark uses occasional guide-led tech; Discovery and Adventure sign a studio technology agreement and use labeled Chromebooks (with home computer access for core work).

## Attendance, Health & Dress Code
- **Attendance:** part-time by design - *if you miss days, it's life, we get it. We don't keep attendance.* A BAND message to your guide covers an absence.
- **Snowy weather:** assume we're in session; a BAND message goes out by 7:30am for any closures; you're the best judge of safety for your family.
- **When to stay home:** fever 100.4F+, vomiting, diarrhea, difficulty breathing, severe cold symptoms, untreated lice, or a positive Strep/COVID test. *Germs are a fact of life; ultimately it's up to you to do what's best for your family.*
- **Dress code:** school-, weather-, and movement-appropriate; nothing depicting alcohol, tobacco, marijuana, or pornography.

## Growth Mindsets + Habits
A big cultural practice at VL is **mindset.** When families and learners adopt growth-oriented mindsets, we see the strongest gains alongside steadier emotions and real joy.

`‹L6 A2›` *This year (2026-2027), our community is reading **The Power of TED** (David Emerald) together. TED teaches that "victim" is not who we are - it's a stance we can step out of. The empowering shift is **Victim → Creator** (the hero mindset), Persecutor → Challenger, Rescuer → Coach. The point is the choice in the moment, not a label on a person.*

| Hero mindset (Creator) | Victim mindset (a stance we shift out of) |
|---|---|
| Welcome challenges; embrace radical responsibility; celebrate excellence; practice kindness; hold firm boundaries; grateful and generous. | Avoid hard work; look for the easy way out; blame, complain, or make excuses - a pattern we can change, not who we are. |

**Partnering for self-governance:** when your child comes home with a struggle, resist the urge to rescue and give growth-minded support - "What did you try? What did you learn? What's your next step?" *Your calm is contagious.*

> *Reflect with us:* When has a failure resulted in some greater lesson for you? Share it with your child. And - what makes a hero a hero?

## Keeping Score: Real Growth, Not Just Grades
We don't "keep score" the way other schools do. **Learners advance by readiness, not by birthdays.**
- **Journey Tracker** (our Hero's Compass app, live this year): the badge plan, work submitted, weekly tracking, and SMART goals.
- **Self-paced mastery:** some finish multiple grade-levels of badges in a year, others take longer - *the only comparison is you, yesterday.* Badges are for mastery, not task completion.
- **Journey Meetings:** learner-led parent-guide conferences, **twice a year (around Sessions 2 and 5)**, to set goals and celebrate growth - ideally with both parents.
- **Moving up:** when readiness thresholds are met, an age-eligible learner prepares a **Pitch to the Next Studio** by spring.

> *Reflect with us (a weekly habit you can share):* What would you do differently? Where were you most challenged? What was your achievement this week - and your failure? When did you serve as a guide to someone, and who guided you?

## Whole Child Development & The Tools We Use
*Grounding, energizing, connecting.* Learners practice simple, evidence-based tools to settle the body and focus the mind: **Brain Gym, Heart Coherence breathing, the Wheel of Awareness, the 4-7-8 breath, EFT (tapping),** and **aromatherapy.** We also keep a **Living Library** of "living books" (Charlotte Mason). *When learners can influence their own nervous system, life becomes a series of choices, not reactions.*

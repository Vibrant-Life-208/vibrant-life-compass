## Why this reopens a sign-off you already gave

On 2026-07-15/16 you (Accord + Jake + TCC/Satis) signed the threshold→slice and
category→slice mappings under a coverage frame with a specific shape: **one aim per slice**,
empty slice = invitation. The captain's 2026-07-16 walkthrough redesign **changes that
shape**. The changes below are the delta that needs fresh eyes — everything else about the
coverage frame is unchanged and still intended to hold.

### The delta (what's new since your sign-off)

1. **A slice is now a LIST, not one box.** Carried thresholds appear **broken out as
   individual goals** (not one lump), first text **locked** (learner can add to a goal, not
   delete or edit the original). The learner **adds up to 2–3 of their own** goals per slice,
   counted across the whole year. Empty slice still = invitation.
2. **Onboarding walks per slice in two passes:** (year: create goals per slice) → (per
   slice: `Now → Year` + write the **halfway goal**). The halfway goal **= the Session 3
   target**.
3. **On the main page, clicking a goal opens a per-goal 3-phase arc** — Set up / Challenge /
   Cross the finish line (= Sessions 1–3); Session 4 = reflection/catch-up, opens later.
   The **year goal is NOT re-shown** when you click in (starts at the halfway).
4. **A "reach the halfway goal by end of Session 3" time-target**, with Session 4 as a
   catch-up buffer.
5. **A live weekly "progressing question"** (phase-aware, asked week by week — not planned
   upfront) → learner writes the week's answer → **a few daily tasks** to reach it. Only
   this-week-and-today is ever on screen.
6. **Two softenings already applied** (need your confirmation they're sufficient):
   - Motivation-independence framed as a **floor, not a whip** ("on a low day, the smallest
     step you can still take") — guards the self-abandonment shadow.
   - "One step more than you want" is a **gift offered after done, never a gate**.

## The specific sign-off questions

### For Jake (developmental fit)
- Does a **list of locked goals + a session deadline** ("reach halfway by Session 3") read as
  a healthy stretch or as pressure/checklist to an 8-year-old?
- Are the two softenings (floor-not-whip; gift-not-gate) **enough**, or do they need more?
- Does the per-goal 3-phase arc honor **exploration-before-commitment** and build
  **mastery-experience / self-efficacy** (easy first week = early win)?
- Is "up to 1 step per week, floor 1" the right grain, or still too much for the age?

### For Accord (coverage frame)
- Multi-goal-per-slice: does **empty-slice-as-invitation** survive when a slice is a *list*?
  Does a pitcher's education slice (several locked goals) read as **load** or as **held**?
- Does the **soft ceiling (2–3/slice)** read as relief ("that's plenty") or as a limit?
- The weekly question + daily tasks cadence — does it stay **presence-shaped**, or does it
  drift toward surveillance/documentary?

### For TCC (data sovereignty — the three binding build conditions)
- **Condition 1 — open-by-choice is a STORED state, never inferred emptiness.** Does the
  multi-goal list preserve "learner chose to leave this open" vs "data missing"?
- **Condition 2 — read-only carried thresholds are read-only to the SYSTEM too.** Is
  "add-to yes / delete + edit-first-text no" a faithful implementation? Nothing silently
  re-maps or auto-completes a declared threshold?
- **Condition 3 — the count never gets a denominator.** Stress-test against the new
  surfaces: "reach halfway by Session 3", weekly steps, daily tasks. Any place a
  "3 of 5 / 3 left / red" can appear = frame breaks.

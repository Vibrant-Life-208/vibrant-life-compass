## MOVEMENT 1 — THE GROUND  *(Kohn: Collaboration; the caring environment)*

**Screen intro**
- **Discovery:** "Before we dream big, let's get your space and your people ready. A few quick things."
- **Adventure:** "Good years start with good ground - the space you learn in and the people around you. Let's set that up."
- **Full:** "Before the goals, the ground. The environment you learn in shapes what's possible. Answer what's alive for you."
- **Parent:** "Before your learner's year, a moment on the ground you can offer them at home."

**Q1 — Reliable space to learn**
- **Discovery:** "Where will you do your best learning? Is it a calm spot you can count on?"
- **Adventure:** "Do you have a reliable space to learn - somewhere you can focus and count on? What would make it better?"
- **Full:** "Do you have a reliable space to learn? What does a space that supports your best work actually need?"
- **Parent:** "Does your learner have a reliable, calm space to learn at home? What's one thing you could offer or protect?"

**Q2 — Accountability buddy**
- **Discovery:** "Who could be your buddy this year - someone who cheers you on and checks in?"
- **Adventure:** "What makes a good accountability buddy? Who could be yours - and could you be theirs?"
- **Full:** "What makes a good accountability buddy - and who will yours be this year? (Real accountability is support, not surveillance.)"
- **Parent:** "Who is your learner's accountability buddy this year, and how can you support that bond without taking it over?"

**Q3 — Open forum**
- **Discovery:** "What makes a group feel safe to talk in - where you can say what you really think?"
- **Adventure:** "What is a real open forum - a space where people speak honestly and get heard? What would make yours safe?"
- **Full:** "What makes a real open forum - where honesty is safe and every voice is heard? What do you need to speak freely, and to help others do the same?"
- **Parent:** *(trimmed - omit, or:)* "What makes a space feel safe for honest conversation at home?"

## MOVEMENT 2 — THE POSTURE  *(TED + Kohn: the empowerment mindset)*

**Screen intro**
- **Discovery:** "How we face hard things matters. Two quick ideas - take what fits."
- **Adventure:** "The mindset you bring shapes the year. Two reflections: how you face challenges, and what it means to open up."
- **Full:** "The posture you bring to the year matters as much as the plan. Two reflections."
- **Parent:** "How you face challenges is what your learner watches most. A reflection or two."

**Q4 — TED mindset** *(Jake, age-scaled — The Empowerment Dynamic)*
- **Discovery:** "When something is hard, you get to choose: stay stuck, or be a Creator who looks for the next small step. When did you last choose to be a Creator?"
- **Adventure:** "The TED mindset: instead of feeling like a Victim of your problems, you can be a Creator (focused on what you want), lean on a Coach (someone who helps you find your own answers), and treat a challenge as something that grows you. Where could you shift from stuck to Creator this year?"
- **Full:** "The Empowerment Dynamic (TED): the Creator (outcome-focused, not problem-focused), the Coach (draws out others' own answers rather than rescuing), the Challenger (calls you forward). Its shadow is the drama triangle - Victim, Rescuer, Persecutor. Where do you want to stand as Creator this year, and where do you slip into Victim?"
- **Parent:** "TED - Creator, Coach, Challenger - versus the drama triangle of Victim, Rescuer, Persecutor. When your learner struggles, how might you Coach (draw out their answer) rather than Rescue (solve it for them)?"

**Q5 — Vulnerability** *(Accord — highest-exposure; gentlest for young; always skippable; never surveilled)*
- **Discovery:** *(gentlest - not "what is vulnerability")* "Sharing a hard feeling - like 'this is tricky for me' - takes courage. What's something real you could share with someone you trust?"
- **Adventure:** "What does it mean to be vulnerable - to let yourself be seen, even when it's uncomfortable? When has being open helped you?"
- **Full:** "What does vulnerability mean to you - the willingness to be seen without armor? Where might letting yourself be seen this year cost something, and be worth it?"
- **Parent:** "What does healthy vulnerability look like to model - showing your learner that being open is strength, not weakness?"

## Author notes (the room)

- **Accord (frightened-body):** Q5 (vulnerability) and Q8 (world-problems) are the two that must pass the exhale test. Q8 is *omitted* for Discovery, not just softened - a seven-year-old should not be asked what keeps them up at night. Q5 for Discovery is reframed away from the abstract ("what is vulnerability") to the concrete and safe ("something real you could share with someone you trust"). Both always skippable; answers are the learner's own and surface on no dashboard.
- **Jake (TED scaling):** the Creator/Victim polarity is the load-bearing, age-portable core - a child can grasp "stuck vs. look for the next step." The full drama-triangle vocabulary (Rescuer/Persecutor) is held back to Adventure+ where it can be understood rather than parroted.
- **Comes (the walk):** three breathing screens, in order Ground → Posture → Self, each a pause, not a form. The learner can move on from any screen without answering - the walk continues whether or not they write.
- **Bareil (container):** one screen per movement holds each as its own small threshold. The skippability line is the open exit on every one.

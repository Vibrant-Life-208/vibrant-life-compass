## 1. The original voice (what to protect)

The playbook's voice is **warm, plain-spoken, first-person "we," directly addressed to "you" and "your learner."** It carries:
- **The sport/game metaphor** as its spine: *Game Setup, Fields of Play, Warm Up, Game Begins, Recess, Keeping Score, Step Up Your Game, Winning the Game.*
- **Hero's Journey + Acton vocabulary:** heroes, calling, tribe, studios, guides, Quests, badges, Journey Tracker, Socratic, exhibitions. Tagline *"Find a calling, change the world."*
- **Small human touches:** "it's a blast," winks and smileys, "we get it." These are part of the warmth - keep a light hand on them, don't scrub them into corporate polish.

**This is the language to stick with.** Most of it should survive verbatim.

## 2. Where the original is ALREADY Level 6 (keep verbatim - do not "improve")

The playbook is, on the whole, strongly learner-sovereignty. These lines are L6 gold and should be preserved word-for-word:

- *"If you miss days, it's life - we get it. We don't keep attendance."*
- *"Since weather can vary widely... we feel you are the best judge of safety for your family."*
- *"Learners advance by readiness, not by birthdays."* / *"the only comparison is you, yesterday."*
- *"This freedom is defined as freedom to work ahead and to work towards independent goals/passions during specified times - not freedom from working..."*
- **3 B's before G** (Brains, Books, Buddy before Guide) - textbook self-efficacy.
- *"resist the urge to rescue... that's where confidence is born."* / *"Your calm is contagious."*
- *"We don't 'keep score' in the same sense that other schools or sports games do."*
- *"we encourage families to be trusting and respectful of each other's choices... ultimately it is up to you to do what is best for your family."*

A Level-6 review's first job here is **restraint**: leave these alone.

## 3. The small Level-6 adjustments (the only proposed changes)

Two meaningful, two light-touch, and a few "keep as-is, already sound." Each shows original -> proposed -> why.

### A1 — ADD the framing promise to the Welcome (additive; the one new element) — RECOMMENDED
The original opens into content without a sovereignty promise. Add Janice's framing-sentence near the top:
> **Add:** *"These are the things we are committed to, and here is why. You do not have to adopt them. You do have to know them, so your choice is a real one. And we will keep thinking about them together."*
- **Why (L6):** positions the parent and learner as Subjects who *choose*, not vessels receiving the school's values (anti-banking). It is additive - it changes none of Jenna's existing words. Highest L6 value, lowest disruption.

### A2 — Anchor "victim mindset" to The Power of TED (keep the term, frame it as a shiftable stance) — RECOMMENDED [REVISED per captain]
> **Original (p52):** a two-column table - *"hero mindset"* vs *"victim mindset"* - where the victim column reads: *"Avoid hard work. Look for the easy way out, seek loopholes... Fail to keep their word, gossip, brag, diminish others. Feel entitled, whine, blame, complain, give excuse for poor behavior."* (The playbook already recommends *The Power of TED* in its reading list.)
> **Captain's direction:** the community is reading **The Power of TED** (David Emerald), which uses "Victim" deliberately - so **keep the word "victim"** and make the parallel to the book explicit, rather than renaming it.
> **Proposed:** keep both column names verbatim. **Add a short anchor line** tying the table to The Power of TED and its core teaching - that Victim is a *role/orientation we can shift out of*, not who we are: **Victim → Creator** (which maps to "hero mindset"), Persecutor → Challenger, Rescuer → Coach. Frame the contrast as a *choice of stance in the moment*, echoing the playbook's own line *"life becomes a series of choices, not reactions."*
- **Why (L6):** this satisfies the sovereignty concern the *right* way - not by hiding the word, but by teaching what the book teaches: the Victim stance is changeable, and the empowering move (Creator/hero) is always available. A child learns the shift, not a label. It also strengthens the community's shared language instead of diverging from it. *Better than the original "rename to stuck mindset" idea, which would have broken the book parallel.*

### A3 — Soften the V-Bucks framing (light touch)
> **Original (p47):** *"Vibrant Life Bucks (V-BUCKS) are the learner-directed accountability system... earned for taking on additional responsibilities, or accomplishing badges or lost by breaking certain agreement... bucks that can be used to purchase items in the v-bucks store."*
> **Proposed:** keep V-Bucks (it's real and on-brand). Lightly reframe from a behavior-control currency toward recognition of responsibility and contribution - e.g., lead with *"earned by taking on responsibility and contributing to the studio"* and treat loss as a natural consequence tied to the studio contract rather than a penalty.
- **Why (L6):** keeps intrinsic motivation in the foreground (the playbook's own value: *"motivation grows from within"*). Minimal wording change; the system stays.

### A4 — Survey "8.5 to stay affiliated" — lead with parent voice (light touch)
> **Original (p60):** *"To remain affiliated with Acton Academy, we must have a 'quarterly survey' average of 8.5 or higher."*
> **Proposed:** keep the fact, but lead with the parent-as-Subject framing already implied elsewhere - *"Your honest feedback shapes Vibrant Life. We hold ourselves to a high bar (an 8.5+ average across the Acton network)..."*
- **Why (L6):** centers the family's voice as something that *changes the school*, rather than a compliance number the school needs from them.

### A5 — Outside Play "3 strikes" — KEEP (already restorative)
- The original already ends in repair: *"they reflect, make a plan, to show they are ready to come back in."* That is restorative, not punitive, and "strikes" fits the sport metaphor you're preserving. **No change recommended** - just noting it passed the L6 scan.

### A6 — "We ask / we request" commitments — KEEP firm
- Per Polaris's gate, do **not** soften the honest costs (parent meetings, availability, gear, Journey Meeting). Inviting language is fine; diluting a real commitment into a suggestion would mislead. **No change** beyond what's already invitational.

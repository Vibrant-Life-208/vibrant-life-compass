## 1. Welcome

> *Janice's framing-sentence as the opening move - the anti-banking promise. This is the spine of an honest condensation.*

Welcome to Vibrant Life.

This playbook is shorter than last year's on purpose. It is meant to help you make one decision well: whether and how your family steps into the coming year with us, and what that will ask of you.

Here is our promise about everything that follows. These are the things we are committed to, and here is why. You do not have to adopt them. You do have to know them, so your choice is a real one. And we will keep thinking about them together, all year.

Vibrant Life is an Acton-model studio. Our work rests on three simple ideas, woven through everything:

- **Learning to Learn** - building the skills and habits to teach yourself anything.
- **Learning to Do** - turning curiosity into real work, real projects, real exhibitions.
- **Learning to Be** - growing the character to use it all well: courage, grit, kindness, honesty.

Our north star is the one line you will see at the bottom of every page: **Find a calling, change the world.**

**A note on our language.** Vibrant Life uses different words on purpose, because the words carry the model. A quick translation:

| Instead of... | We say... |
|---|---|
| Student / Teacher | Learner (or Hero) / Guide |
| Classroom / Classmates | Studio / Tribe mates |
| Grades / Report cards | Mastery, Badges & Exhibitions |
| Worksheets / Textbooks | Quests, mentors & real-life experiences |

## 2. The decision in front of you

You are not enrolling in a class schedule. You are choosing a way of learning for your child and a way of participating for your family. This playbook walks the decision in the order the questions usually come:

- Which studio fits my child this year? *(Section 3)*
- What does a day actually look like, and what are the hours? *(Section 4)*
- How does my child grow and move up? *(Section 5)*
- What will this ask of me as a parent? *(Section 6)*
- What does progress look like, so I will know it when I see it? *(Section 7)*

Read those five, and you will have what you need to decide. The rest of the playbook is here for once you are in.

## 3. Fields of Play: which studio is my child in?

> *The heart of the deliverable. Faithful to source, condensed to a decision aid: age, the belief, what they Do, who they Become.*

Learners are grouped into studios by readiness, not strictly by age - so the ages below are a starting point, not a rule (more on that in Section 5).

| Studio | Generally ages | In one line |
|---|---|---|
| **Spark** | 5-7 | Curiosity and purposeful play growing into self-directed work |
| **Discovery** | 8-10 | Purposeful challenges and choices that build an independent learner |
| **Adventure** | 11-14 | Choice plus accountability that turns curiosity into a disciplined craft |
| **Launchpad** | *(planned)* | Know who you are, trust your capability, design your next great adventure |
| **Next Great Adventure** | 15-18 | Graduate with a portfolio, mentors, field hours, and a real Launch Plan |
| **P&T (Parents & Tots)** | youngest | An introduction-level program; a gentle first taste of the community |

### Spark - generally ages 5-7
- **What they do:** A strong reading foundation (Waseca), early handwriting and writing, number sense with Montessori math works, nature and science, and multi-week Quests with little to no technology. They plan and carry out their own exhibitions.
- **Who they become:** Kind, courageous, responsible; a good friend; able to collaborate in a tribe, work through conflict, and use simple tools to manage big feelings.

### Discovery - generally ages 8-10
- **What they do:** Quests of 4-6 weeks that blend science, art, math, and writing into a real project with a public exhibition. A writing workshop cycle (draft, peer critique, revise, publish), deep reading, Khan-based math, and story-rich history with Socratic discussion. They begin a respectful relationship with technology through the Journey Tracker portal.
- **Who they become:** A learner who sets goals and owns results, celebrates smart failures, manages conflict, speaks in public, shares opinions, and cares for the studio and each other.

### Adventure - generally ages 11-14
- **What they do:** Quests of 4-8 weeks in STEM, arts, and entrepreneurship, with real rubrics and public exhibitions. Writing workshops, primary-source history with claim-evidence-reasoning, science labs tied to Quests, and short apprenticeships or service hours with reflection. Accomplishments are logged as badges in the Journey Tracker.
- **Who they become:** A young person who takes on servant-leadership roles, gives and receives feedback, holds peers accountable to contracts, journals and pitches their thinking, and works with real grit.
- *Good to know:* a learner can stay in Adventure longer if the next studio is not yet established. Valuable learning happens either way.

### Launchpad *(planned to open in the future)*
- **What they do:** Mastery across core subjects, shown through drafts, defenses, and real audiences; scientific and analytic thinking; intentional technology; and work alongside mentors in fields they want to pursue, including real internships.
- **Who they become:** Purposeful, high-integrity, with habits of excellence and full ownership of their results and impact.

### Next Great Adventure - generally ages 15-18
- The capstone years. Learners graduate with a professional portfolio, mentor references, meaningful field hours, and a public Launch Plan - college, trade, venture, service, or a gap year - all backed by evidence of what they can actually do.

### P&T (Parents & Tots)
- An introduction-level program, so it does not carry everything the older studios do, and many parts of this playbook will not apply yet. Parent meetings happen during program time, and P&T families are warmly invited to family activities and Thursday field trips. It is a gentle first taste of the Vibrant Life community for your youngest.

## 4. A day at Vibrant Life: the rhythm and the hours

> *The literal "hours" question. This is what a family is committing its time to.*

Vibrant Life is **part-time by design.** As we put it: we really power-pack the time when we are in studio. That shapes everything about the commitment, so it is worth understanding before you decide.

**The shape of a day** (exact times vary a little by studio):

| Time | What happens |
|---|---|
| 8:30 - 8:45 am | Outdoor play and drop-off (all weather, outside) |
| 8:45 - 9:00 am | Brain Gym - movement to wake up the body and mind, plus announcements |
| 9:00 am onward | **Work Block** - the heart of the day (ends late morning for younger studios, early afternoon for older) |
| Midday | Lunch and outdoor play - eaten outside, with extended free play; studios closed except for the bathroom |
| ~1:50 - 2:00 pm | Pickup; the day closes at 2:00 pm |

**Inside the Work Block**, younger studios move through morning circle, a tea ceremony, nature exploration, imaginative and collaborative play, read-aloud, snack, and gratitude. Older studios run morning circle, town hall, goal-setting, then a rotation through reading, math, writing, Socratic discussion, Quest time, communication challenges, and a closing reflection with character call-outs.

**On attendance - read this one closely, it shapes the commitment:**

> "With VL being part-time we really power-pack the time when we are in studio. If you miss days, it's life - we get it. We don't keep attendance. When a learner is out it's their responsibility to check in with their studio and make a plan."

So the commitment is less about perfect attendance and more about showing up ready to use the time fully. Plan family trips for long weekends or break weeks where you can. When a learner is out, a quick BAND message to the guide is all that is needed.

**Weather:** assume we are in session, rain or snow. A BAND message goes out by 7:30 am if anything changes. You are the best judge of safety for your own family, and we trust that judgment.

## 5. How a learner grows and moves up

> *Corrects the most common misread - that studios are grade levels. They are not.*

At Vibrant Life, **learners advance by readiness, not by birthdays.** This is true in Acton studios around the world.

A learner moves up when clear thresholds are met - habits of self-governance, completed goals, and demonstrated mastery - and when they have genuinely grown into the next stage. When an age-eligible learner is ready, by May they prepare a **"Pitch to the Next Studio,"** showing real evidence of their growth through badges and reflections.

And if the thresholds are not yet met? No problem. The path simply continues until readiness comes. No child is rushed, and no child is held back as a punishment. The question is always *"are they ready,"* never *"how old are they."*

**How growth is tracked.** Instead of grades, learners earn **badges** - mastery credentials backed by real, publishable work, not prizes for finishing. Each learner has an annual badge plan, co-created with you and the guide, visible in the online **Journey Tracker** (badge plan, weekly progress, SMART goals). Learning is **self-paced**: some learners finish several grade-levels of badges in a year, others take longer, and that is fine - the only comparison is you, yesterday. Mastery sometimes takes more than a year, because we would rather a foundation be solid than rushed.

**How learners get unstuck.** A signature Vibrant Life habit is **"3 B's before G"** - before asking a Guide, a learner tries their own **Brains**, available **Books** and resources, and a **Buddy**. Only then do they ask a guide, who answers with leading questions rather than the answer. It builds the self-reliance the whole model rests on, and it is something you can reinforce at home.

## 6. What Vibrant Life asks of you as a parent

> *The honest family commitment - load-bearing for a real decision. This is what "yes" actually costs.*

Choosing Vibrant Life is a family commitment, not a drop-off. Here is what we ask, plainly:

- **Be reachable during the day.** Guides are with the learners; we may need you for a bathroom need or a moment of extra emotional support, especially for younger learners.
- **Join the community.** We are a community and your gifts matter. We ask families to join a volunteer committee and complete the Skills Form so we know what you would love to offer.
- **Come to parent meetings.** We hold quarterly meetings on a topic of growth for you as a person and a parent. We ask you to attend at least three of the four.
- **Show up for the annual Journey Meeting.** Once a year, you, your learner, and the guide meet to set goals, make plans, and celebrate growth. Ideally both parents attend. You can request more meetings any time.
- **Partner, do not rescue.** When your child comes home with a struggle, the invitation is to coach rather than fix - curious questions over quick answers ("What did you try? What will you try next?"). Resisting the urge to rescue is how confidence is born. As we put it, your calm is contagious.
- **Bring an all-weather mindset.** Much of the day is outside, on purpose, in every season. The invitation is to cultivate a "there's no bad weather" outlook alongside your child.
- **Provide the gear and the device.** Outdoor layers for all weather are expected (they need not be new - thrift and hand-me-downs are encouraged). Discovery and Adventure learners also need a labeled Chromebook for studio work and computer access at home for core work.
- **Tend yourself, too.** Being a Vibrant Life parent can be stretching - joyful and hard - as we make space for the healthy struggle that grows confident, independent people. Take care of yourself, lean on the community (Book Nooks, Mom Meet-Ups, Dad Campfires), and keep learning alongside your child in ways that fit your season.

**How we will communicate.** Use the BAND app for absences, quick questions, and emergencies; email the guides (vibrantlife208guides@gmail.com) for meetings and longer questions; and ask for a meeting with the owner for anything sensitive. Guides stay present with learners during the day, so expect a reply within three days during the session. You can always reach Jenna during the day.

**If it is ever not the right fit.** We work hard to resolve issues through conversation first. If a family is considering leaving, we ask for at least two meetings with the guide and two with Jenna to try to repair or adjust before withdrawing. If a transition is still the right step, the current month's tuition is prorated and refunded. We part ways, when we must, with grace and mutual respect.

> *We do family life together - on purpose and with joy.*

## 7. What progress looks like here

> *So a parent knows results when they see them - without traditional grades.*

Vibrant Life does not hand out traditional report cards. Progress shows up in three visible ways:

- **Exhibitions of Learning** - "like living report cards." At the end of each session, learners showcase what they have made and learned. Exhibitions are planned entirely by the learners, and guides intentionally do not step in, even when something goes sideways - because the exhibition itself is the learning. Families are warmly invited.
- **Character call-outs** - we regularly stop to name the courage, kindness, grit, and honesty we witness in each other. When learners are seen for who they are becoming, motivation grows from within.
- **Badges** - accomplishments and mastery, logged in the Journey Tracker, that build toward a learner's pitch to the next studio.

## 8. The everyday rhythms

> *Condensed reassurance, not exhaustive policy. Detail moves to onboarding.*

- **Food & water:** healthy eating is a life skill here. Send nourishing food and a labeled water bottle each day; we go lighter on sugar without making it a "never." We respect that families hold different dietary philosophies.
- **Nature & play:** outdoor time and real play are central at every age, with room for managed risk - tree climbing, bare feet, muddy boots. We let learners assess their own abilities and risk levels, so scrapes and bruises are treated as a normal part of play; you will not hear from us about every boo-boo, only about anything serious. As we like to say, encourage your child to come home with grassy, sandy feet; that is the childhood they deserve.
- **Technology as a tool, not a toy:** studio time is social-media-free and game-free, with safety filters on devices. The real aim is to coach learners to make good choices online. P&T is screen-free; Spark uses occasional, guide-led technology; Discovery and Adventure sign a studio technology agreement.
- **Health & illness:** please keep your child home for the symptoms listed in the onboarding guide. Our stance is trusting and respectful of each family's own judgment - germs are a normal part of building a healthy immune system, and you decide what is best for your family.
- **A simple environment:** studios are kept calm and uncluttered, so we ask that toys and books stay home (except for self-organized show-and-tell). Shoes-off is welcome inside.
- **What to bring:** a labeled water bottle, lunch and snack, a backpack, and weather-appropriate layers.

## 9. Tools we use to help learners self-regulate

> *Brief signal that the program is regulation-first / trauma-informed. Research citations compressed out.*

Each morning, and throughout the day as needed, learners practice simple, evidence-based tools for settling the body and focusing the mind: **Brain Gym** (cross-lateral movement), **Heart Coherence** breathing, the **Wheel of Awareness**, **4-7-8 breathing**, tapping (EFT), and calming aromatherapy. The aim is steady: when learners can influence their own nervous system, life becomes a series of choices rather than reactions.

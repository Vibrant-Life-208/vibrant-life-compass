# Vibrant Life Playbook - Video Storyboards v0.1 (Cartoon / Animated)

> Tool-agnostic storyboards for the four videos. Drop these into Canva, Powtoon, or Animaker. Built from the v0.1 scripts; cartoon/illustrated style.
>
> **Voice plan:** v1 uses **tool TTS** (warm, natural voice). Plan a **re-record pass later** - Jenna for the Welcome and What-We-Ask pieces, and **consider learner narrators** for "A Day" and the studio shorts (a learner describing their own studio is powerful and on-brand; needs parental consent).
>
> **Consistency rule:** build the Cast & World below **once**, reuse across all four videos *and* pull the same illustrations into the PDF for one unified look. This is what stops character drift.

---

## CAST & WORLD BIBLE (build once, reuse everywhere)

**Characters**
- **Maya the Guide** - warm adult guide, calm posture, kneels to learner eye-level (never lectures from above). Represents all guides; recolor/restyle slightly per studio short if you want distinct guides.
- **Spark learner** (~6) - bright, curious, often barefoot/outdoors.
- **Discovery learner** (~9) - busy with a project, goal chart nearby.
- **Adventure learner** (~12) - leading a small group, presenting/pitching.
- **Teen learner** (~16) - portfolio/laptop, mentor nearby (Launch Pad, coming soon).
- **Tot + parent pair** (P&T) - small child with a caregiver.
- **A parent** - appears in the "what we ask" beats (volunteering, pickup, meeting).

**Settings**
- **The Studio** - calm, uncluttered, warm light; reading nook, project tables.
- **The Outdoors** - grassy field, trees (two small "practice trees"), all-weather sky that can switch sun/rain/snow.
- **The Roundabout** - drop-off/pickup.
- **Recurring motifs** - badges as small medal/star icons; the Journey Tracker as a friendly screen; a peacock feather (celebrations).

**Studio colors (per captain 2026-06-28; use for each studio's card/short/lower-third):** Tots = **orange**, Spark = **yellow**, Discovery = **purple**, Adventure = **green**, Launch Pad (coming soon) = **blue**. Order youngest-first. Next Great Adventure is folded into Launch Pad.

**Style notes** - bright, friendly, simple (Animaker-ish), not corporate-slick. The bar from the room: *"she pictures her child here," not "what a nice video."* Language tracks **v0.6** - Welcome opens on the real mission; Tots short = **Tots Tribe** (ages 3-4); **"A Day" re-aimed** to land growth-without-grades (badges, exhibitions, "you, yesterday").

**Tagline card** (reusable end card) - *Find a calling, change the world.*

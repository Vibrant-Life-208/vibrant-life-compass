## Welcome  ‹L6 A1›

Welcome to Vibrant Life - we're so glad your family is here.

*These are the things we are committed to, and here is why. You do not have to adopt them. You do have to know them, so your choice is a real one. And we will keep thinking about them together, all year.*

Vibrant Life is an Acton-model studio. Everything we do rests on three ideas: **Learning to Learn** (the skills and habits to teach yourself anything), **Learning to Do** (turning curiosity into real work, real projects, real exhibitions), and **Learning to Be** (the character to use it all well - courage, grit, kindness, honesty).

Find a calling, change the world.

## Game Setup

### Attendance Policy & Illness
With VL being part-time we really power pack the time when we are in studio. Please try to plan family trips on long weekends or break weeks. If you miss days, it's life - we get it. We don't keep attendance. When a learner is out it's their responsibility to check in with their studio and make a plan, especially for any Quest roles that depend on them.

- **Absences:** Send a BAND message to your guide. Feel free to CC Jenna.
- **Late arrival:** No need to message, just come when you can.
- **Early pick up:** Best practice: tell your learner in advance so they know to be ready.
- **Snowy weather:** Assume we are in session. A BAND message will be sent out by 7:30 am if there are any closures. Our basic philosophy is, we will be in session, and it's up to each family to decide what they will do. We feel you are the best judge of safety for your family. Often we have combined play time during snowy days - it's a blast.
- **When to stay home:** please keep your child home for fever (100.4F+), vomiting, diarrhea, difficulty breathing, severe or colored-discharge cold symptoms, untreated lice, or a positive Strep/COVID test. *Germs are a fact of life and can assist in healthy immune system development... we encourage families to be trusting and respectful of each other's choices, and ultimately it is up to you to do what is best for your family.*

*(Parking, the full illness list, medications, and emergency plans live in the onboarding guide.)*

## Fields of Play

*Learners are grouped into studios by readiness, not strictly by age.*

**Parents & Tots (Tots)** *(our littlest)*
- P&T is an introduction-level program, so it won't have everything the older programs have, and many items in the Playbook won't relate to these young learners yet. P&T Parent Meetings are held during program time. P&T families are invited to family activities and Thursday program-wide field trips. We hope you enjoy the unique connection and community that the P&T tribe offers!

**Spark** *(generally ages 5-7)*
- *Guiding Belief:* Curiosity and purposeful play & growing into self-directed work.
- *Learning to Be:* Practice kindness, courage, and personal responsibility; learn to be a good friend; collaborate with a tribe; emotional regulation tools.
- *Learning to Learn & Do:* Strong reading foundation with the Waseca program; handwriting and early writing; number sense with Montessori math works; nature and science; multi-week Quests with little to no technology. Plan and carry out exhibitions.

**Discovery** *(generally ages 8-10)*
- *Guiding Belief:* Purposeful challenges + choices grow independent learners and a strong sense of self.
- *Learning to Be:* Set goals and own results; celebrate smart failures; practice conflict management; independence with community; public speaking; care for personal items and studio; form and share opinions; join healthy Socratic discussions.
- *Learning to Learn & Do:* Quests (4-6 week real-world projects blending science, art, math, and ELA, with public exhibition); writing workshop cycle (draft, peer critique, revise, publish); deep reading and DEAR; online Khan math; story-rich Civilization with Socratic dialogue; a respectful relationship with technology via the Journey Tracker.

**Adventure** *(generally ages 11-14)*
- *Guiding Belief:* Choice + accountability turn curiosity into a disciplined craft. Learners stretch into independent work and community leadership.
- *Learning to Be:* Servant-leadership roles; team building and conflict resolution; give and receive feedback; goal setting and peer accountability to contracts; creator mindset ("What's my plan?"); reflective journaling and public speaking (pitches and exhibitions); grit and confidence.
- *Learning to Learn & Do:* Math intertwined with Quests, games, and Khan; ELA writing workshops and communication challenges; Civilization with primary sources and claim-evidence-reasoning; science labs tied to Quests; Quests (4-8 week STEM/arts/entrepreneurship with public exhibitions); short apprenticeships and service hours with reflection; badges logged in Journey Tracker. *(A learner can stay in Adventure longer if the Launch Pad studio is not yet established. Valuable learning happens either way!)*

**Launch Pad** *(generally ages 15-18 - coming soon)*
- *Guiding Belief:* Know who you are, trust your capability, and design your next great adventure.
- *Learning to Be:* Purpose and servant leadership; high-trust integrity; habits of excellence; own your results and your impact.
- *Learning to Learn & Do:* Mastery across core subjects shown through drafts, defenses, and real audiences; scientific and analytic thinking; intentional tech; mentors and real-world internships.
- *The next great adventure:* graduate with a professional portfolio, mentor references, meaningful field hours, and a public Launch Plan - college, trade, venture, service, or gap year - backed by evidence of what you can do.

## Daily Life at Acton

**How a day goes** *(times vary a little by studio):*
- **8:30-8:45 am** Outdoor play and drop-off (all weather).
- **8:45-9:00 am** Brain Gym - we meet at the grassy field for cross-lateral movements designed to integrate the brain's hemispheres and enhance learning, plus announcements and a wellness exercise.
- **9:00 am - Work Block (Game Begins)** Morning circle, music, and town hall, set goals; then a rotation through reading, math works, writing workshop, Socratic Launch, Quest time, communication and improv challenges, closing circle, reflection, and character call-outs.
- **Midday - Lunch & Outdoor Play** Eat lunch with your child outside; all weather; studios closed except for bathroom or first aid.
- **Pick-up by 2:00 pm.** Pickup at the roundabout starts at 1:50.

**What to bring:** labeled water bottle, lunch and snack, backpack, and a change of clothes / weather layers.

**How We Start the Day** *(8:45-9:00, grounding, energizing, connecting):* The Morning Exercise Leader says "Good morning!" and the learners reply "Yes it is!" We move through Brain Gym, Heart Coherence, and the Wheel of Awareness.

## Your Hero's Journey

Being a VL parent can be stretching as we make space for healthy struggle so learners grow into confident, independent people. It can be joyful and hard. Our invitation: take care of yourself, connect with the community, and keep learning alongside your child in ways that fit your season. The more the core principles make sense, the easier it is to notice the small wins and trust your hero's - and your own - capacity to grow in meaningful ways.

### Partnering for Self-Governance
"The first and best victory is to conquer self." When a learner comes home with a struggle and looks to you, we ask that you respond in a way that empowers them. Often as parents we want to rescue; when we resist the urge to rescue and instead give growth-minded support, it shows them we have faith in their ability to choose, try again, and own the result. That's where confidence is born. Use questions like, "What did you try? What did you learn? What's your next step?" **Your calm is contagious.**

## Additional Tools We Use
*Grounding. Energizing. Connecting.* Learners practice simple, evidence-based tools to settle the body and focus the mind: **Brain Gym**, **Heart Coherence** breathing, the **Wheel of Awareness**, the **4-7-8 breath**, **EFT (tapping)**, and **aromatherapy** (lavender for calm, rosemary for memory). We also keep a **Living Library** of "living books" (Charlotte Mason). *When learners learn to influence their nervous system, their capacity grows and life becomes a series of choices, not reactions.*

## Studio Systems & Getting to Know the Vocabulary

- **Studio Contracts:** a list of promises learners make to each other; each learner signs and agrees to hold one another accountable. It's the main governing document for the learners.
- **Guardrails:** it is the guide's responsibility to maintain the guardrails and keep a physically and emotionally safe environment.
- **V-Bucks:**  ‹L6 A3›  Vibrant Life Bucks are a learner-directed system for recognizing responsibility and contribution - earned by taking on responsibilities and accomplishing badges, and tied to the studio contract the tribe sets for itself. They can be used in the v-bucks store. *(Introduced when a studio is ready, and the system evolves each year.)*
- **Badges:** a badge is a mastery credential - clear evidence (publishable work) that a learner met a defined standard. It's not a prize. Each learner has an annual badge plan co-created with parents and guide, reviewed in Journey Meetings, worked at their own pace.
- **Town Hall** and **360 / 4A Feedback:** learner-led governance and warm-and-cool feedback at the end of each session.

### 3 B's Before G
Before asking a Guide, a learner first uses their **Brains** (define and attempt the problem), their **Books** (studio resources, approved online tools, a community expert), and a **Buddy** (work jointly to understand, not just to get the answer). Only then do they ask a Guide - not for the answer, but for leading questions toward it. This grows self-efficacy and perseverance.

### Open Forums (Communication That Heals)
Every conflict is a doorway. In an Open Forum we use nonviolent communication (Marshall Rosenberg) and the simple flow **OFNRR**: what did you Observe ("I noticed..."), Feel ("I felt..."), Need ("because I needed..."), Request ("would you be willing to...?"), and what could you be Responsible for ("I could..."). Each person reflects back what they heard, then they high-five, shake, or hug.

## Mindset + Habits

A big cultural practice at VL is the principle of mindset. When families and learners adopt powerful, growth-oriented mindsets, we see the strongest academic gains alongside steadier emotions, a more empowered presence, and real joy.

‹L6 A2›  *This year (2026-2027), our community is reading **The Power of TED** (David Emerald) together. TED teaches that "victim" is not who we are - it is a stance we can step out of. The empowering shift is **Victim → Creator** (the "hero mindset" below), Persecutor → Challenger, and Rescuer → Coach. The point is the choice in the moment, not a label on a person.*

| Hero mindset (Creator) | Victim mindset (the stance we shift out of) |
|---|---|
| Welcome challenges and embrace radical responsibility. Strive for and celebrate excellence. Praise heroic choices by others. Practice frequent acts of kindness and hold firm boundaries. Grateful and generous. | Avoid hard work; look for the easy way out or loopholes; fail to keep your word; blame, complain, or make excuses. |

*Embrace the Hero's Journey - choose creatorship over victimhood. Mistakes are information, not identity.* If this is not yet a strong place for your family, consider reading *The Power of TED* and *Mindset* (Carol Dweck) together.

## Keeping Score

We don't "keep score" the way other schools or sports games do. Learners set goals and track progress as a tool to grow.

- **Journey Tracker:** the studio's badge plan, work submitted, weekly tracking, and SMART goals all live here. *(This is our first year using it more fully, so please be patient as we learn it together.)*
- **Self-Paced Mastery:** learners work at their own pace toward academic badges, with the expectation that they stay steadily engaged. Some finish multiple grade-level badges in a year; others take longer. We invite parents to embrace this rhythm: **the only comparison is you - yesterday.** Badges are earned for mastery and excellence, not task completion.
- **Journey Meetings:** once a year, families are offered a Journey Meeting with the Guide, the learner, and ideally both parents, to assess goals, make plans, and celebrate. You may request additional meetings any time.

## Alignment & Transitions (Parting Ways)

We work hard to keep communication open and protect a healthy studio culture; most issues resolve with conversation and support. If a family is considering leaving, we ask for at least two meetings with the guide and at least two with Jenna, to try to repair or adjust first. If withdrawal remains the best path, current-month tuition is prorated and refunded. We value every learner and family - even when paths diverge, we ask all parties to proceed with grace and mutual respect.

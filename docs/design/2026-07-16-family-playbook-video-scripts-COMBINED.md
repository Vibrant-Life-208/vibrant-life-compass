# Vibrant Life Family Playbook 2026-2027 - Companion Video Scripts (COMBINED)

> **What this is:** the four companion-video scripts for the Family Playbook, combined into one working document. Base is the **v0.2 additive cut** (2026-06-30 panel, approved) - the "show, model, or carry feeling" version, not the recite-the-page version.
>
> **The rule for every script here:** if a line could be lifted straight off the v0.6 playbook page, it doesn't belong in the video. Each video ADDS something not on the page, and ends by pointing back to the playbook. v0.1 stays as the *content guarantee* (what must be true); this is *how to show it.*
>
> **Style:** cartoon/animated; studio colors as set; four-video ceiling held. TTS for a rough cut; Videos 1 and 4 especially want the **real re-record** (Jenna's warmth; modeled dialogue) - TTS is a placeholder there.

## VIDEO 1 - "Why Vibrant Life" (~70s)
**Voice: JENNA SAYS (Jenna's real voice for re-record).**

*ADDS: Jenna's personal **why** + one real "becoming" moment + warmth. Does NOT recite the mission - the mission words live on page 1. Best with Jenna's real voice.*

**[0:00-0:14]**
- **VISUAL:** A child stuck on something - a puzzle, a tricky line of a book - hesitating, glancing around for help.
- **VOICE (Jenna):** "I'm Jenna. I started Vibrant Life because I kept watching bright kids learn one quiet lesson a little too well: *wait to be told.*"
- **CAPTION:** *Why we exist*

**[0:14-0:34]**
- **VISUAL:** The same child decides to try it themselves - works at it - and gets it. The small, real light-up on their face.
- **VOICE:** "I wanted a place where a child learns the opposite. That they can find the answer. Lead the room. Own their own growth. That look - right there, when they figure it out without being rescued - that's the whole thing."
- **CAPTION:** *It's about becoming.*

**[0:34-0:55]**
- **VISUAL:** Quick warm glimpses of the community - a tribe outside, a guide kneeling beside a learner, families at an exhibition.
- **VOICE:** "We're not here to do school to your child. We're here to help them become someone who can find a calling and change the world - and to walk it with your whole family."
- **CAPTION:** *We walk it with your family.*

**[0:55-1:10]**
- **VISUAL:** Tagline resolves.
- **VOICE:** "Our mission and our promise to you are on the very first page of the playbook. This is the feeling behind those words. Take your time with it - we'll think about it together."
- **CAPTION:** *The mission is on page 1. This is the heart of it.*

> **Bridge:** points to the playbook's Mission/Welcome page rather than repeating it.
> **L6:** poses (invites, doesn't sell); valued (leads with the child's becoming and Jenna's why, not a hook).

## VIDEO 2 - "A Day, and How Growth Works" (~80s)
**Voice: LEARNERS READ (learner-narrator; needs parental consent).**

*ADDS: the rhythm **in motion** + a **real exhibition shown** (the growth model demonstrated, not stated). Does NOT re-read the schedule - times stay on the page. Light VO; let the day breathe.*

**[0:00-0:08]**
- **VISUAL:** Black-to-warm open on an empty studio at 8:25, then learners arriving.
- **VOICE:** "Words on a page can't quite show you a day here. So watch one."
- **CAPTION:** *A day at Vibrant Life*

**[0:08-0:34] - mostly visual, ambient, minimal VO**
- **VISUAL:** A flowing montage - Brain Gym on the grass; morning circle; a Quest project taking shape; two learners working a problem out *without* a guide jumping in; lunch outside, muddy boots.
- **VOICE (sparingly, ~once):** "Outside, in their bodies, then deep in real work. We power-pack the time."
- **CAPTION:** *(let it breathe)*

**[0:34-1:00] - the heart: a real exhibition**
- **VISUAL:** A learner stands to present at an exhibition - nervous, then finding their footing. The guide stays seated, doesn't step in. Parents and tribe watch. A badge moment.
- **VOICE:** "This is how we 'grade.' Learners show what they've actually made, to people who care - no red pen, no report card. Some move quickly, some take longer. The only person they're racing is who they were yesterday."
- **CAPTION:** *No grades. Real work, shown. You, yesterday.*

**[1:00-1:15]**
- **VISUAL:** Roundabout pickup, a tired-and-proud learner.
- **VOICE:** "That's a day. They leave a little more themselves."
- **CAPTION:** *The full rhythm & calendar are in your playbook.*

> **Bridge:** the schedule and calendar live on the page; the video shows the *feeling* and the *exhibition* the page can only describe.
> **L6:** poses (shows how growth works, lets the parent judge); valued (honest, centered on the child, no ranking).

**Shared shape:** guide waves and introduces themselves → "here's a real moment from our studio" (one specific example) → "what we're about is in your playbook - here's a peek inside."

### Tots Tribe (3-4 · ORANGE)
- **VISUAL:** Guide at a nature circle; a tot beside a parent in a quiet "Safe Base" goodbye at the door.
- **ON-SCREEN:** *Tots Tribe · a peek inside*
- **VOICE:** "I'm [guide]. Here's a morning in the Tots Tribe - we follow the child's play, and parents grow right alongside. Last week a little one said goodbye at the door all by herself for the first time. That's the work. What we're growing, and how you grow with us, is in your playbook."

### Spark (5-7 · YELLOW)
- **VISUAL:** A Spark learner mapping "our community"; the tribe planting a spring garden; a flash-forward to a small debate in front of parents.
- **ON-SCREEN:** *Spark · a peek inside*
- **VOICE:** "I'm Ms. Megan, in Spark. Our Quests follow the seasons. Right now the tribe is mapping our own community and starting a garden for spring. And later this year, these five- to seven-year-olds will hold a real debate, in front of you. Even at five, they're driving. What Spark is all about is in your playbook."

### Discovery (8-10 · PURPLE)

- **ON-SCREEN:** *Discovery · a peek inside*

### Adventure (11-14 · GREEN)

- **ON-SCREEN:** *Adventure · a peek inside*
- **VOICE:** "I'm Ben, in Adventure. Watch one of our learners run our studio's Town Hall - they propose the rules, they hold each other to them. That's what 'learner-led' actually looks like. The full picture is in your playbook."

### Launch Pad (15-18 · BLUE · coming soon)
- **VISUAL:** A blue horizon; a teen with a portfolio + a "Launch Plan" page; a "coming soon" banner.
- **ON-SCREEN:** *Launch Pad · coming soon*

## VIDEO 4 - "Partner, Don't Rescue" (~60s)
**Voice: JENNA SAYS (Jenna's real voice; modeled dialogue).**

*ADDS: a **modeled before/after scene** of the one commitment that changes most at home. Does NOT re-list the parent commitments - the full list stays on the page. The whole point is showing the **how**, which a page can only name. Best with real voices.*

**[0:00-0:10]**
- **VISUAL:** Jenna, or a warm narrator, to camera.
- **VOICE:** "We ask real things of families - they're all in the playbook. But there's one that changes the most at home. Let us show you, instead of just telling you."
- **CAPTION:** *The one to practice first*

**[0:10-0:28] - the rescue**
- **VISUAL:** A child at the kitchen table, frustrated. "I can't do my project, it's too hard." A parent slides in: "Here, let me just do this part for you." Child slumps back, hands it over.
- **CAPTION:** *The rescue*

**[0:28-0:48] - the partner**
- **VISUAL:** Same scene. The parent pauses, sits beside them: "Hmm. What have you already tried? What's one small next step?" The child thinks, picks up the pencil, tries.
- **CAPTION:** *The partner*

**[0:48-1:00]**
- **VISUAL:** The child, a little later, showing the parent what they figured out.
- **VOICE:** "Same love. Different move. We call it partnering, not rescuing - and it's how confidence is born. Your calm is contagious."
- **CAPTION:** *Everything we ask is in your playbook. Start here.*

> **Bridge:** the commitments list stays on the page; the video models the one that's hardest to *do.*
> **L6:** poses (shows a choice, doesn't shame the rescue instinct); valued (names it "same love," honors the parent).

## The three principles

### 1. NO SORTING — every path whole (PDC)

Every Discovery learner gets a whole, honoring year, whatever their path (mover-up / stayer /
reached-and-fell-short / first-year fresh-start). The flow must never make one path the "real" one.
Age is **timing, never tier** — "not yet" must never become "not enough." The fresh-start of a
first-year learner is kept whole; it is the highest-leverage moment, not a deficit to manage.

*Check:* does this surface make any path feel like the leftover, the consolation, or the deficit
version of another? If yes, it fails.

### 2. THE APP NEVER SCORES — the un-sorting room (SSC)

The app is never the pitch's scorekeeper. The school ranks; the app **witnesses**. The app only ever
tracks *growth* — it never stakes the year on pass/fail and never frames a threshold as a bar to
clear. There is therefore no "fell short" state in the app to mishandle. The wound stays where it
lives (the committee room, human-held). The app is the one place in the learner's day that does not
sort them at all. **Floor-and-ceiling:** the app is safe *alone* (no configuration or absent adult
can produce shame or a verdict) and steps back to *support the human* when a guide or parent is
present. Parent **approval → witness** (nothing to approve *against*, so an approval flow cannot
become a weapon).

**Verdict vs. reflection (sharpened 2026-07-18, PDC research cycle).** "Never scores" means never a
*verdict* — never a rank, a pass/fail, a percentage, or a comparison to another learner. It does
**not** mean never reflecting the learner's own competence back to them. Self-determination research
(need-support meta-analyses, combined N in the hundreds of thousands) finds **competence is the single
strongest driver of self-directed motivation**, and it is fed by *informational, self-referenced*
feedback — the learner's own prior words and work shown back: "here is what you built, learned, can now
do." So the growth-mirror is not a tolerated exception to this principle; it is the principle's
**positive obligation**. The failure mode is symmetrical: a surface that scores the learner *sorts*
them (the wound we know), and a surface that reflects *nothing* *starves* the competence that motivates
them (the wound we were about to build). Reflect competence; never grade it. And the same research names
the cost of getting it wrong on the parent side: monitoring a child reads to them as *distrust of their
competence*, which measurably lowers autonomy and raises anxiety — so a parent scorecard is not only a
principle breach, it is a measured harm. *(Refs: `2026-07-18-pdc-research-brief.md`.)*

*Check:* can any configuration, path, or outcome cause this surface to deliver a verdict, a rank, or
a comparison? If yes, it fails. **The growth-mirror is path-blind and outcome-blind — it literally
cannot tell the four paths apart.**

*Check (positive obligation):* does this surface reflect the learner's own competence back to them —
informationally and self-referenced (compared only to their own past), never against a bar or another
learner? Informational and self-referenced = keep. Evaluative or comparative = kill. A surface that
reflects nothing fails this check as surely as one that grades.

### 3. THE ROOM NEEDS A MYTH, NOT JUST A MERCY — the belonging-spine (MAC)

Removing the sorting is necessary and **insufficient**. A restraint is not a home; an un-narrated
un-sorting room reads as the ladder with the app not helping. You cannot beat a story with a policy.
So the room is furnished with **one belonging-story every learner is inside: Root · Tend · Deepen.**
The climb is real and lives in the world (down the hall, worth pride); the app **witnesses** it
warmly but **never narrates** it. Carried thresholds arrive as **roots, not rungs.** And the myth
**ranks itself above achievement no more than below it** — the ground does not resent the ladder;
sort in *neither* direction. *We are not the softer ladder; we are the ground it stands in.*

*Check (two-way voice rule):* does this string rank achievement over belonging, **or** belonging
over achievement? Either direction fails. Does it consecrate a belonging, or measure an achievement?
It should consecrate.

## How the principles cash out in surfaces (from the MAC review)

- **One narrative spine** (Root · Tend · Deepen) for all four paths; the pitch/climb is never staged
  inside the app.
- **Three honest rites** — Welcome/Arrive · Set-the-Year · the Mirror — on the four bones (entry /
  consecrate / witness / exit). The app *sets the table, never blesses the meal*; it holds a place
  for a human witness and never impersonates one.
- **Two path-myths:** returning-stayer = *Deepen*; first-year = *Arrive* (a welcome, not a "refusal"
  burden).
- **The growth-mirror:** the learner's own prior words, unannotated, beside where they are now;
  nothing from the app between them; grounding beat first; entered not pushed; un-anticipated (never
  advertised); doubles as the year's Return / ending.
- **Witness ≠ narrate:** the app holds the climber's joy if brought ("that's yours, I'm glad")
  without a badge, verdict, or plot.
- **Symmetry of richness:** same beats, weight, and warmth across branches; a blank naming is
  scaffolded example-first so an empty box is never a void.
- **Rest door = true peer:** the two doors ("one small step / rest") are visual and motion peers;
  neither is primary; nothing rewards "step" over "rest."
- **Vocabulary-teaching (first-year):** example-first, *ordinary* examples (Tuesday, not
  hero-examples), the welcome teaches the language while it plans.

## Standing gates (do not flip the flag until these hold)

- **Watch-with-a-real-learner** must include a *staying* learner AND a *first-year* learner — not
  only a pitcher (PDC).
- **SSC flag-flip safety lock:** the flag does not flip for any cohort that could contain a
  pitch-and-fall or a first-year learner until the third-learner soft landing AND the newcomer floor
  exist and are walked. (Salus's CONTRAINDICATED rating is a lock on exposure, not a note.)

**Date:** 2026-06-27

**Research:** live WebSearch, June 2026 (AI video tooling moves monthly; verify again before purchase)

## The decision that frames everything

"AI generate these videos" can mean three very different things, and they are *not* interchangeable for a real school selling to real parents:

| Path | What it makes | Fit for this project |
|---|---|---|
| **A. AI avatar + voice of the real people** | A *Jenna* avatar (her likeness + voice, with consent) reading the script; same for guides | **Strong** - it's authentically *them*, just generated delivery |
| **B. AI-assist on real footage** | Edits/captions/voiceover over footage you already have | **Strong for b-roll** - generates nothing fake |
| **C. Fully generative scenes** (Veo, Kling, Runway, Sora) | Synthetic children, a synthetic "studio," a fabricated "day" | **Fails the Level-6 gate** - see below |

**Why Path C is the wrong tool here.** The whole creative direction from the meeting was *"real studios, she pictures her child here,"* and the Level-6 gate is *"valued or used."* Showing parents **AI-fabricated children** to represent a real, community-built school is a misrepresentation - it asks them to feel something about kids who do not exist. That is "used," not "valued," and it is a credibility risk for a school whose entire pitch is authentic community. Generative scene tools are extraordinary technology aimed at the wrong problem for this deliverable.

**The likely real driver for going AI: summer timing.** School is out. You probably *cannot film a real day or real learners before 7/30.* That is a legitimate reason to reach for AI - but it argues for **Paths A and B** (generate the *people's words*, edit *real existing assets*), not for fabricating a fake school.

## The tool landscape (current, June 2026)

### Path A - AI avatar / voice of real people

- **HeyGen** - the realism leader in 2026 (Avatar IV: lifelike lip-sync, micro-expressions). Makes a **custom avatar ("digital twin")** of a real person and a **voice clone**, both with a built-in consent flow (upload a consent video + liveness check; written consent required for any third-party voice). States **100% of user data is not used for model training.** Pricing: **Creator plan ~$29/mo** (unlimited videos, 1080p, voice cloning included); custom-avatar slots **+$29/mo each**; voice clone **~$99/yr**; Pro ~$99/mo adds 4K. One caveat from security researchers: HeyGen's consent checks are *slightly looser* than Synthesia's.
- **Synthesia** - more corporate/training-oriented, **stricter consent/governance**, but avatars read slightly more "synthetic." ~$18/min or fixed tiers from ~$18/mo (limited minutes). Better if governance/audit trail matters more than realism.

### Path B - AI-assist on real footage (generates nothing fake)

- **Descript** - text-based editing (edit the transcript, the video updates), auto-captions, AI voiceover. Built to *polish footage you already have*, not invent footage. Ideal if you have last year's photos/clips to assemble into "A Day."
- **Canva** - auto-captions + AI voice generator, and **it's already where the playbook master will live.** Tight integration: assemble video, caption, export, *and* produce the PDF in one place. Lowest new-tool overhead.

### Path C - fully generative scenes (for reference; not recommended here)

- **Google Veo 3.1** - best all-rounder, native synced dialogue, 4K.
- **Kling 3.0** - cheapest premium (~$0.10/sec), multi-shot consistency.
- **Runway Gen-4.5** - most creative control.
- **Sora - being discontinued** (web/app Apr 26 2026, API Sep 24 2026). **Do not build on it.**
- Use only for *abstract/illustrative* motion (e.g. an animated tagline card), never to depict actual VL children or the studio.

### Privacy note (it's a school)

Education-AI guidance in 2026 stresses COPPA/FERPA, data retention, and that some models train on children's data. Practical rules for us: **get consent for any real person's likeness/voice** (HeyGen's flow covers Jenna and guides); **don't upload identifiable children's footage** into tools that train on your data; **minimize** what you upload; and have someone review output for honest, inclusive representation.

**Why this stack:**
- It clears the deadline without filming live (summer-proof).
- It stays authentic - real people's words and likeness, real existing imagery - so it passes the Level-6 gate the room set.
- It's cheap: **~$29/mo HeyGen + Canva** (which you'll have anyway for the PDF). No per-video scene-generation costs.
- It keeps one tool (Canva) as the hub for *both* the PDF and the video assembly, so the playbook and its videos stay in sync.

**If you have zero usable real footage for "A Day":** ship videos 1-3 by 7/30 and film a real "Day" in the fall when learners are back. A genuinely real day-in-the-life is worth more than a synthetic one, and the meeting's video ceiling (four) and "real not slick" bar both support waiting on the one piece that needs real kids.

## Level-6 gates still apply to AI

The Janice and Polaris tests carry straight over:
- **An AI Jenna posing the questions** = fine (her words, her likeness, consented). **An AI fabricated child** = "used, not valued."
- Disclose nothing dishonest: if a voice is cloned or an avatar is used, that's a production choice, not a deception, *as long as it's the real person's own words and consented likeness.* The line we don't cross is inventing people or moments that imply something false about the school.

# ADDENDUM (2026-06-27) - Cartoon / animated direction

**Captain's redirect: go cartoon/animated rather than realistic.** This is the cleanest answer to the whole tension above, and the research supports it.

## Why cartoon is the *right* call here, not a compromise
- **It dissolves the authenticity problem.** An openly illustrated style cannot be mistaken for the real studio, so it cannot misrepresent it. The Level-6 "valued or used" gate is satisfied by design - no fabricated "real" children, because nothing pretends to be real.
- **It makes "A Day" filmable now.** The one video that needed real summer footage becomes a cartoon day-in-the-life. The summer-timing blocker disappears entirely - all four can ship by 7/30.
- **It fits the audience.** Education research cited in 2026 reports cartoons improve retention and simplify complex ideas; it reads warm and family-friendly, on-brand for a children's school.
- **It can unify the whole deliverable.** A consistent illustrated "Vibrant Life world" (the studio, the outdoors, the tribe, a guide character) can live in *both* the videos and the PDF - one visual identity across the entire playbook.

## The one real challenge: character consistency
The known failure mode of AI cartoon is **character drift** - generic AI video tools forget what your character looks like between scenes. Two ways to beat it:
- **Template character platforms** (Animaker, Vyond, Powtoon) keep a consistent cast natively - you build/pick characters once and reuse them. Best for a multi-video series like ours.
- **Anchor-frame workflow** (build the character once, generate a pose/expression set, feed those into a video tool) - more work, only needed if you want a custom-illustrated look a template can't give.

## Cartoon tool options (June 2026)
| Tool | Best for | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Powtoon** | Quick template explainers | Largest template library; script + template AI; between animation and motion graphics; affordable tiers |
| **Animaker** | Accessible character cartoons | Library characters/scenes, drag-and-drop, **built-in AI dubbing + auto-subtitles**; bright friendly cartoon look; affordable |
| **Vyond** | Maximum control/quality | Fully scripted character acting; **pricier (~$699/yr Starter)** - likely overkill for this |
| **Canva** | Lowest overhead | Animation elements + characters + AI voice, and **it's already your PDF hub** - one tool for everything, though lighter on true character animation |
| **Pika 2.5** | Stylized generative 2D | Good for branded 2D explainer motion without prompt engineering; character consistency still harder than template tools |

## Revised recommended stack (cartoon)
- **Do all four in one template tool** so the cast and the "VL world" stay consistent across the series: **Powtoon or Animaker** (pick on aesthetic - Animaker leans bright/friendly, Powtoon leans template-fast). Check current tiers before buying; both are far below Vyond's price.
- **Or stay in Canva** if its animation depth is enough - lowest overhead, one tool for PDF + videos + captions. Worth a quick test first; if the character animation feels too thin, move to Powtoon/Animaker.
- **Voice: use Jenna's real voice** - record the script on a phone, drop the audio over the cartoon. Cartoon visuals + Jenna's actual voice = warm, authentic, and summer-proof, without any avatar/likeness questions at all. (Tool TTS is the fallback if recording is a hassle.)
- **Build a small reusable cast once** (a guide character, a couple of learner characters, the illustrated studio + outdoors) and reuse across all four videos - and consider pulling the same illustrations into the PDF for one unified look.

## Updated decision fork
1. **Tool:** start in **Canva** (test its animation) or commit to **Powtoon/Animaker** for stronger character work?
2. **Voice:** Jenna records the scripts (recommended), or tool TTS?
3. **Visual identity:** want one illustrated "VL world" shared across videos *and* the PDF? (Recommended - it's a free brand unifier.)
4. Want me to **adapt the four scripts into storyboard form** (per-scene: illustration description + on-screen text + VO line) so they're ready to drop into whichever tool you pick?

- *Learning to Learn & Do (academics):* Strong reading foundation with the Waseca program; handwriting and early writing; number sense with Montessori math works; explore nature/science; multi-week Quests with little to no technology; plan and carry out exhibitions.

**Discovery Learners - generally ages 8-10**
- *Guiding Belief:* Purposeful challenges + choices grow independent learners and a strong sense of self.
- *Learning to Be:* Curiosity; work-hard/play-hard; set goals and own results; celebrate smart failures; conflict management; independence with community; public speaking and sharing ideas/voice; clean and care for personal items and studio; form and share opinions; healthy Socratic discussions.

**Adventure Learners - generally ages 11-14**
- *Guiding Belief:* Choice + accountability turn curiosity into a disciplined craft. Learners stretch into independent work and community leadership.
- *Learning to Be:* Servant-leadership roles; team building and conflict resolution; give/receive feedback; goal setting, peer accountability to contracts; creator mindset ("What's my plan?"); reflective journaling and public speaking (pitches/exhibitions); deeper sense of self and passions; clean and care for the studio; grit and confidence.

- *Note:* "A learner can stay in Adventure longer if the LP studio is not yet established. Valuable learning happens either way!"

**Launchpad Learners** *(planned to open in the future)*
- *Guiding Belief:* Know who you are, trust your capability, and design your next great adventure.
- *Learning to Be:* Purpose and servant leadership; high-trust integrity; habits of excellence; own your results and your impact.

**Next Great Adventure - generally ages 15-18**

## ADDITIONAL TOOLS WE USE `[CONDENSE]`
*Why CONDENSE: reassuring proof of the trauma-informed/regulation approach, but the research citations are deep detail - keep the list, compress the science.*
*| grounding. energizing. connecting. |*

- **4-7-8 Breath (relaxing):** inhale through nose for 4, hold 7, exhale with a soft "whoosh" for 8; four cycles. Calms for transitions/big feelings. (Dr. Andrew Weil. Practiced once a week during Wednesday Morning Exercises; learners built up from a 3-4-6 breath.)
- **EFT / Tapping:** tap acupressure points while naming feelings to process emotion. (Linked to reduced anxiety, better focus, emotional regulation in young children.)
- **Aromatherapy (lavender + rosemary):** smell + three slow breaths; lavender for calm, rosemary for memory/alertness. (Scent reaches the limbic system fast.)
- **Heart Coherence:** slow breathing while focusing on something loved or appreciated; brings the nervous system into balance. (HeartMath Institute.)
- **Wheel of Awareness:** stand, breathe, place attention in the "hub," move a "spoke" to notice body, senses, inner feelings/thoughts, then connection with others; ends with a kind wish. (Dr. Dan Siegel.)
- **Brain Gym:** gentle cross-lateral movements that activate both brain hemispheres; supports concentration, balance, cognition.
- **Living Library** (Charlotte Mason): curate "living books" that carry rich ideas; minimize "twaddle." Shelves lean toward books that shape character, vocabulary, imagination.
- **Toddler Developmental Support (P&T):** tools/ideas from Emma Hubbard plus the emotional-regulation tools above.

### Studio Systems & Vocabulary (p47) `[KEEP]` (badges) / `[CONDENSE]` (systems)

                       4–7–8 BREATH (RELAXING BREATH)

                       Learners inhale quietly through the nose for 4, hold for 7, then exhale with a soft “whoosh” for 8,
                       repeating four gentle cycles. The extended exhale cues the parasympathetic system, creating quick
                       calm for transitions, big feelings, or winding down.

                       Popularized by Dr. Andrew Weil, research on paced breathing with longer exhales links brief daily
                       practice to improved heart-rate variability, emotion regulation, and reduced stress reactivity—
                       supporting steady focus and self-control. Learners do this practice one time a week during their
                       Wednesday Morning Exercises and also are invited to use it throughout the day if or when needed. To
                       build up, they have begun with a 3-4-6 breath for the first couple of months.

                       EMOTIONAL FREEDOM TECHNIQUE (TAPPING)
                       Tapping on specific acupressure points while naming what we’re feeling helps learners process
                       emotions and shift into a more peaceful state.

                       Studies show EFT is effective in reducing anxiety, improving focus, and
                       building emotional regulation skills, especially in young children.

                       AROMATHERAPY (LAVENDER + ROSEMARY)
                       Learners smell lavender (for calm) or rosemary (for memory/alertness) and take three slow breaths while
                       noticing body and mood. Odor signals travel from the nose    →  to the olfactory bulb →  enter the primary
                       olfactory cortex and limbic areas (amygdala/hippocampus) without a first stop in the thalamus, which
                       means scent influences emotion and memory quicker than other sensory input. Other sensory input has
                       to stop at the thalamus, the relay station, before moving to the next stop of the brain for processing.

                       Research: Lavender inhalation shows reductions in anxiety/stress, while rosemary aroma is linked to
                       better memory/working memory, with the rosemary correlating with an increased cognitive
                       performance in studies.

                       Living Library: We believe literature can feed the heart and soul. Following Charlotte Mason’s lens, we
                       curate “living books”—well-told works that carry rich ideas—while minimizing “twaddle” texts that talk
                       down or are thin of thought. Both entertainment and nourishment have a place, but our shelves lean
                       toward the books that shape character, vocabulary, and imagination. Our libraries draw on Charlotte
                       Mason, Well-Educated Heart, and other trusted sources. Modern research shows that U.S. schoolbooks
                       and literature started getting simpler around the mid-1900s, with clear declines from the 1930s through
                       the 1990s. This happened because publishers aimed for readability scores, and responded to
                       testing/policy pressures that favored shorter, easier excerpts over rich, whole texts.

                       Toddler Developmental Support: For P & T studio, we use tools and ideas from Emma Hubbard, the
                       emotional regulation tools listed above, as well as others.

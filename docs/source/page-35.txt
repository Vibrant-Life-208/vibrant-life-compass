                       Why prioritize nature & play at all stages of development?
                                        Some profound studies and resources on the value of nature & play are:
                        Balanced and Barefoot by Angela Hanscom, Last Child in the Woods by Richard Louv & Dr Gordon Neufeld

                             “Play allows children opportunities to get creative, to practice regulating emotions, to enhance social
                                          development, and learn about themselves in the process” Angela Hanscom

                        “An environment-based education movement--at all levels of education--will help students realize that school
                              isn't supposed to be a polite form of incarceration, but a portal to the wider world.” Richard Louv

                         “Children literally thrive by challenging their bodies. When their bodies aren’t challenged, they fall behind in
                                                               their development.” Angela Hanscom

                        “Play is nature’s secret agent, clearly disguised, enabling the more visible characters in the story to fulfill their
                                                                   purpose.” Dr Gordon Neufeld

                             “The woods were my Ritalin. Nature calmed me, focused me, and yet excited my senses.” Richard Louv

                        “Children with health neurological systems naturally seek out the sensory input they need on their own. If they
                           are jumping off a rock over and over, it is because they are craving that sensory input.” Angela Hanscom

                        “Time in nature is not leisure time; it's an essential investment in our chidlren's health (and also, by the way, in
                                                                       our own).” Richard Louv

                        “We used to think that schools built brains. Now we know that it is play that builds the brains that school can
                                                                 then use.” Dr Gordon Neufeld

                       “It is important to realize that child need to challenge their bodies in various ways several hours a day, and to do
                                                          so with little adult direction.” Angela Hanscom

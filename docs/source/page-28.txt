                               HOW WE START THE DAY AT VIBRANT LIFE
                                          | grounding. energizing. connecting. |
                          Each morning at Vibrant Life, we take a few intentional minutes from 8:45-9:00 am for our
                        morning exercises. These are to help learners settle into their bodies, empower their minds, and
                                                  connect with themselves and each other.
                        The Morning Exercise Leader begins by saying “ Good morning!” the learners reply: “Yes it is!”


                     BRAIN GYM
                     We start with gentle, coordinated movements that activate both sides of the brain and
                     prepare the body for learning. These exercises improve concentration, balance, and
                     cognitive performance.

                                  Research supports it: Movement that crosses the body’s midline helps
                                  strengthen neural pathways and improves attention in young learners.

                     HEART COHERENCE
                     Learners are guided to breathe slowly while focusing on something they love or feel
                     grateful for. This brings their nervous system into balance and fosters a grounded, calm
                     state.

                                 HeartMath Institute research shows that heart-focused breathing
                           increases emotional resilience, reduces stress, and enhances decision-making.

                     WHEEL OF AWARENESS
                     Learners stand, breathe, and place attention in the “hub,” then gently move a “spoke” to
                     notice the body, the five senses, inner feelings and thoughts, and finally connection with
                     others—ending with a kind wish. This builds ownership of our awareness, calm focus,
                     and a felt sense of belonging.

                                   Developed by Dr. Dan Siegel, this practice improved attention, emotion
                                 regulation, and social connectedness, with reductions in stress reactivity.

                     CHARACTER CALL-OUTS
                     We regularly build in character call-outs to name and celebrate character — the
                     courage, kindness, grit, and honesty we witness in each other. This creates a culture of
                     recognition and deepens connection in our learning community.

                       When learners are seen for who they’re becoming, motivation grows from within.
